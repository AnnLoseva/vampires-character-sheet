# Vampire: The Masquerade 5th Edition — Reference Archive

## Introduction

### Mina Harker's Letter

Dear Alex,

If you have no wish to know all that I want to tell you, burn this letter for your own good. Forgive me this melodramatic opening, which would do Mr. Stoker credit, but I assure you the matter is of the utmost importance. And this concerns not only this letter, but also our recent meeting, which, as you have probably already understood, was by no means a romantic rendezvous.

"Villa" did indeed have hidden motives, but probably not the ones you expected or hoped for. In truth, what brought me to you was a matter of life and death — and of the turning of one into the other. I would never have approached you, neither under my own name nor as "Villa," were it not for two immutable truths, inseparably bound together.

First, death awaited you within a couple of decades of agony. Doctors would sooner or later have found a flaw in your blood and, having no way to learn the truth, would have given the affliction a name fitting the era. Your mother was once diagnosed with "Chediak-Higashi syndrome," your grandmother's authoritative Zurich specialists found "severe thalassemia," and Quincey's great-great-grandfather slowly died of "chronic myeloid leukemia."

Second, the man — the **Beast** — who defiled your ancestors' blood is no more. I believe that this time he truly has met his end — perhaps at the hand of some modern Lord Godalming draped in digital apparatus, or perhaps (and I still hope for this) he died as all living things die, asleep in the rays of the sun, in harmony with the Creator.

When we first overcame him, it seemed to me as though his shadow had dissolved, and I decided then that his blood had died with him. I learned the truth only after Quincey was born — a child to whom, unknowing, I had passed the cursed blood. And yet, though nightmares plagued me and fear whispered of the inevitable in my ear, the truth was revealed to me in a letter much like the one you now hold. He was still alive, and his hold over my soul had not weakened in the least.

His letter was an attempt at confession, but a threat ran through every line all the same. He told me that he had followed my son's and grandchildren's lives "with the greatest interest." And I understood that I could do nothing to help them while his interest had not waned. That inaction, for the time being, was the better course.

My resolve weakened without doubt, as I watched my children and grandchildren fade before my eyes, poisoned by his blood. I watched them fight their own flesh, suffer beneath the sun's rays, and shy from the comfort of the Almighty. And I could not ease their burden even in the smallest way by explaining the cause of their afflictions, for that would have placed both them and their children in even greater danger.

I must give him his due — in this, he proved better than I. He told me — at considerable risk to himself, as I soon realized — of my new nature and of the dark underside of the world to which my folly and his greed had led me. I burned his letter after reading it. Even in those days, when I still walked beneath the sun, my memory was flawless, and I judged that the document would expose us both to needless risk.

But circumstances have now changed. His blood now flows in your veins too, and I believe it is only right to pass on to you his knowledge as well, supplemented by my own observations where they differ. By the time you read this, the pain of the **Embrace** will already be behind you, and you will, I suspect, already know the torment of your new **Hunger**.

If horror films have not entirely passed you by, you have surely already guessed, at least in broad strokes, what has happened to you. Your assumptions are largely correct, though the sources from which you might have drawn them are, at best, incomplete — unsurprisingly. Let us nonetheless give the screenwriters their due, for even our own sources are often contradictory, and quite possibly contain falsehoods born of malice rather than ignorance.

For many reasons you may have already guessed, it is my duty to help you discern truth from fiction.

Forever not yours,  
**Wilhelmina Harker**

### What Kindred Are (in Brief)

Let me begin, perhaps, with what many of us are eager to boast of: crosses, garlic, wolfsbane, running water, and other folk remedies are nothing more than fairy tales, and they cannot harm us. But I have seen with my own eyes how **Kindred** (as we call ourselves) recoil before sacred symbols, even though, it would seem, nothing stands behind them but the pitiful remnants of human superstition.

And just as few are willing to admit that stress and hunger drive them to strange behavior: like characters from some fairy tale, they begin counting grains, stop short at running water, or flee from white-colored animals. Indeed, the most experienced among us prefer to avoid animals of any color, unless we know a sure way to calm them: beasts smell the scent of death we give off, and so bare their teeth at us or try to flee as far as they can.

#### Vulnerabilities

Imagined dangers can only harm those who believe in them, but there are things every Kindred truly has cause to fear:

- **A wooden stake through the heart** — ordinary steel or bullets can only paralyze a Kindred. We sink into deep **torpor**, from which we will not wake soon (perhaps decades, if our blood is thick and strong).  
- **Beheading or burning** will kill a Kindred as surely as it would any mortal.  
- **Fire** consumes our flesh whether we are paralyzed or not.  
- **Sunlight** — elders suffer the torments of hell beneath its rays, while some of the youngest, unfledged neonates, known as **thin-bloods**, can walk outside by day and get little more than a mild sunburn.

### History and Blood

Despite all the advantages of youth, standing in Kindred society can only be earned with age. As blood passes from **sire** to progeny and generation follows generation, it grows ever thinner, ever weaker.

1. **Caine:** According to legend, he was the first Kindred, cursed twice over: for the murder of his brother and for the pride he showed in founding his city.  
2. **Second Generation:** Caine sired three progeny.  
3. **Third Generation (the Antediluvians):** They had a hand in destroying their own sires and consolidated their power after the Great Flood. From them descend the **Clans**, which pass on their progenitors' traits.

Over time the Antediluvians withdrew into shadow and became legend. In these nights, some Kindred consider their very existence nothing more than a heretical myth. I myself am rather more fond of the story of the **Dark Mother, the sorceress Lilith**, who taught Caine the art of alchemy, by which he changed the blood of his children.

#### The Curse of Time

The sins of the fathers weigh on us less and less from generation to generation, and this is at once both our blessing and our curse:

- Elders need more blood, and it must be human (animals do not sate them).  
- With age, Kindred grow set in their convictions and lose the capacity to adapt.  
- They lose their memories of mortal life. And with them goes everything human — not only pity or love, but also a sense of proportion and the capacity for empathy. Having lost their connection to the world, they become easy prey for a starving **Beast**.

### The Beast and the Hunger

The **Beast** and the **Hunger** — these are your eternal companions. If you say the Beast is nothing more than a convenient excuse for our sins, a kind of projection of the subconscious or whatever psychologists call it these days, this will not trouble me in the least. No, I will even be glad for you, for it means your soul has not yet felt the heat of its breath.

When you first betray your **Humanity**, breaking your own rules to satisfy some monstrous, momentary urge (and this will happen very soon) — only then will you be able to form your own opinion as to whether the Beast exists. Every Kindred remembers the first time they found themselves in the Beast's grip. All of us dread the night when nothing will be able to loosen its hold, when we will not manage to break free and become ourselves again.

The inevitability of the fall, the thought that sooner or later each of us will lose control, gnaws at our minds like a rat chewing through a ship's rigging, turning immortal existence into a mocking torment.

Such is our curse: we must satisfy our **Hunger** with blood to keep the Beast on its leash. To live like a beast, so as not to become one — that is our lot. Our hunger combines the agony of one starving for food with an eternal need to prove something to someone, and the despair of an abandoned child. Blood is desire, a narcotic more potent than opium, a needle sharper than cocaine's. It drives us mad and recedes only once we have drained another victim to the last drop. And even then it will return the moment the sun sets — again and again, until the end of time.

### The Properties of Blood and the Disciplines

Blood does not merely sate the Hunger. It feeds emotions and sharpens abilities. Having drunk the blood of a melancholic, we feel our passions quiet, and our ability to go unnoticed grows stronger. Each Antediluvian left their descendants priceless gifts: a **Nosferatu** can vanish right before your eyes, a **Ventrue** can hypnotize with a single glance. My blood carries these gifts too — and just as my sire could turn to mist, the boundaries of our very physiology blur under outside pressure.

### Blood Bonds and the Embrace

Blood unites us. First and foremost, we are bound to the one who gave us the **Embrace** — our **sire**.

- **Creating a Kindred:** The sire must first drain a mortal's body, then touch their lips with their own **vitae** (our blood). If all goes well, the corpse will cling to the wound and be reborn as a childer.  
- **Servants:** If a living person tastes vitae, they will all but stop aging and will gain strength, but will pay for it with their own will, becoming a slave to their master.  
- **Blood Bonds:** It is said that one who drinks another Kindred's blood three times will fall under a bond as strong as that between a sire and their progeny.

### Diablerie

If one Kindred drains another completely, they are not ensnared — on the contrary, they grow stronger. Rulers (Princes or Barons) condemn this act of fratricide, calling it **diablerie**. Whoever commits it must hide their deed from everyone, or else **Final Death** awaits them.

### Sects and the Eternal Struggle

Kindred are forever mired in betrayals and conspiracies, calling this the **Eternal Struggle** (Jyhad).

- **The Camarilla:** A worldwide secret organization, founded in the age of the Renaissance. Its purpose is to uphold the **Masquerade** (concealing our existence from humanity). The Camarilla's elders and Princes demand unquestioning obedience.  
- **Anarchs:** Those who reject the Camarilla's tyranny. Among them are many factions: from those hungry for power to those who simply want to be left alone.  
- **The Sabbat:** A faction mired in sin even by vampire standards. Now elders hear the **Call**, drawing them east to fight the Sabbat in the **Gehenna War**.

#### The Second Inquisition

Today the chief threat is not some mythical apocalypse, but the government agencies of mortals. The Vatican, the United States, and Great Britain have formed an alliance known as the **Second Inquisition**. They burn our havens and hunt our trail with modern technology. The Anarch uprising tore a hole in the veil of the Masquerade, and now we are prey once again.

### A Final Word

Will you join the **Camarilla**, these refined overlords? Or will you side with their opponents — the **Anarch Movement**? The choice is yours. I know too well what bondage is to compel you.

And one last request. I think you have already guessed who I am. I did not only give you the Embrace — I once gave life to your ancestor — I am your great-great-grandmother. Distorted versions of our history first appeared on the pages of a novel, and then on cinema screens. I leave these materials to you. Study them and decide for yourself what to do with this burden.

You will not see Villa again. The only thing I ask: if you despise me for this curse, never seek a meeting with me. We must be as free as our nature allows us to be.

Forever not yours,  
**Wilhelmina Harker**

### Game Ethics and Safety at the Table

In *Vampire: The Masquerade*, you will be playing as something evil. Characters must hunt people and drink their blood — and that alone is already a crime. They may sink further still — perhaps having to kill to survive. And if they do not renounce vampire society, sooner or later they will be made to stand and watch as ancient monsters commit unspeakable atrocities.

As a Storyteller, you will naturally want to sustain an atmosphere of horror, but at the same time avoid disgusting your players so badly that they refuse to keep playing. **Everyone has their own threshold of what is acceptable.**

**Discuss with the rest of the troupe in advance the lines that must not be crossed.** Don't pressure players or try to talk them out of their limits. It's better simply to take each participant's individual sensitivities into account. A scene of bloody torture might seem perfectly acceptable to one player, while that same player is categorically opposed to depictions of domestic violence.

Tabletop role-playing games are valuable precisely because players have the ability to shape the game from the very start, tailoring it to themselves.

#### Useful Safety Tools

| Technique | How It Works |
|----------------------|--------------|
| **Lines and Veils** | **Lines** are themes that are never brought up at all. **Veils** are events that happen "off-screen," without detailed description. |
| **Fade to Black** | A player or Storyteller says "Fade to black" — and the scene immediately cuts to the next one. |
| **Stoplight** | Colored cards: Green — all good, Yellow — proceed carefully, Red — stop. |
| **X-Card**          | A sheet of paper with a large letter "X." Touch it, and the scene ends immediately, no explanation needed. |
| **Open Door**   | Any participant may step away from the game at any moment, with no consequences. |

#### Post-Session Debriefing

After a session, it's useful to spend 10–15 minutes to:
- Step out of character (speak about your character in the third person).
- Share impressions: what stood out, what was difficult.
- Release emotional tension.

**The cardinal rule:** character ≠ player. Everything that happens at the table is a fictional story. It does not reflect your real views and must not cause harm to any participant.

## 02. The World of Darkness and Kindred Society

### History and Legends

In the **World of Darkness**, all manner of strange creatures hide among humanity, vampires among them. They look human, try to act human, but do not be fooled. Night sets everything in its place: **the vampire is a predator, and the human is its prey**.

The World of Darkness is not some distant fairy-tale realm. Vampires are right next door. **The World of Darkness is our reality, our time. Here and now.**

#### The Legend of Caine

According to the oldest traditions, the first Kindred was **Caine** — son of Adam and Eve, who out of envy killed his brother Abel. God laid two curses upon him:

- **The Curse of Blood** — Caine must forever slake his thirst with the blood of the living.
- **The Curse of the Beast** — an untamable predatory instinct took root inside him, one that over time devours what remains of his humanity.

Caine wandered the earth for a long while, until he founded the first city — **Enoch**. There he tried to build a society equal to the lost Eden, and in his pride sired three progeny, thereby defying God's will. For this he was cursed a third time — with the **curse of fatherhood**: his children and all their descendants would carry his sins and the eternal struggle within them.

#### The Second Generation

Caine created three children, known as the **Second Generation**. Their names are usually unspoken or hidden in the legends. They were immensely powerful, almost equal to their sire. They became the first true rulers among the Kindred.

According to tradition, the Second Generation eventually rebelled against Caine, or were destroyed in the course of ancient wars. Almost all knowledge of them has been lost or deliberately erased.

#### The Third Generation — the Antediluvians

After the Great Flood, three (or more) of the Second Generation's progeny rebelled and destroyed their sires. These beings became the **Antediluvians** — the progenitors of all modern clans.

Each Antediluvian passed a portion of their essence and their own personal curse on to their descendants. This is how the first **Clans** came to be. Over time, the Antediluvians withdrew into shadow or fell into a millennia-long torpor. For most modern Kindred, they are nothing more than myth or a frightening tale. Yet elders still fear that one day the Antediluvians will awaken and demand their legacy back.

#### The Alternative Legend — Lilith

Not all Kindred believe in the story of Caine. Many (especially among sorcerers and mystics) revere **Lilith** — the Dark Mother, Adam's first wife, cast out of Eden.

In this version, Lilith found Caine after his exile and taught him **the art of changing blood** — what would later become the foundation of all the Disciplines and Blood Sorcery. She is said to have given him a power even God did not possess. Some bloodlines (especially the Tremere and certain cults) hold that Lilith, not Caine, is the true progenitor of vampirism.

#### The Curse of Generations

With every new generation, the blood grows weaker. Elders need ever greater quantities of human blood, and lose flexibility of mind and memories of mortal life. The farther one is from Caine, the weaker the bond to the ancient curse — but the weaker the power as well.

### The Eternal Struggle (Jyhad)

Kindred are forever mired in betrayals, intrigue, and wars, which they themselves call the **Eternal Struggle** — the **Jyhad**. This is not merely a war for power. It is an eternal conflict between generations, between the ancient and the young, between those who wish to preserve the old order and those who hunger for change.

#### The War of Ages: Young Against Old

| Generation     | Worldview                              | Relationship to the World          |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Elders / Methuselahs** | Think in terms of centuries and millennia   | Conservatism, paranoia   |
| **Neonates and Ancillae**      | Live in the modern world                   | Technology, rebellion, fury |

Elders are gradually departing — many hear the **Call** (the Beckoning) and set off for the Middle East, leaving their domains to the young. For neonates this is an opportunity; for the Camarilla, a crisis.

#### Great Milestones of the Jyhad

- **The Punic Wars** — a clash between the Ventrue and Malkavians (Rome) against the Brujah (Carthage).
- **The Albigensian Crusade** — the intrigues of the elder clans.
- **The Fall of Constantinople** — diablerie committed upon the "Archangel" Michael.
- **The French and October Revolutions** — young Kindred used mortal chaos to overthrow their sires.
- **The Modern Age** — the Second Inquisition and the Beckoning have finally broken the old balance.

#### The Current State: The Final Nights?

Many believe that **Gehenna** has come — the end of the world for the Kindred.

- **The Beckoning**: Vampires older than the 8th generation feel an irresistible pull to leave for the Middle East.
- **Elder Paranoia**: The ancients who remain drink the blood of their own progeny so as not to lose their minds.
- **The Rise of the Neonates**: Cities increasingly fall under the control of young coteries.

For many unaligned vampires, this whole "Apocalypse" is simply a convenient excuse the elders use for their personal vendettas.

**The Jyhad never ends.** It only changes shape: from open war to shadow intrigue, from swords to digital surveillance and political coups. Every Kindred sooner or later becomes a participant in it, whether they wish to or not.

### The Sects

The Kindred have never been a single people. From the very start they split into rival factions — the **sects**. Each offers its own answer to the question: "How do we survive and rule in a world that hates us?"

Today, four powers remain the main players:

#### The Camarilla (the "Ivory Tower")

The oldest and once the most powerful sect. Founded in the age of the Renaissance as an alliance of the elder clans to protect the **Masquerade** and preserve the feudal order.

- **Ideology**: Law, hierarchy, stability. "We are the shepherds of humanity."
- **Structure**: Rigid feudalism. The Prince is the absolute ruler of the domain, the Primogen is the council of clans, the Sheriff and Harpies provide enforcement and social control.
- **Today**: Has lost many domains (London, Berlin, Los Angeles). Many elders have departed on the Beckoning. Now forced to hand more power to neonates, lest it collapse.

#### The Anarch Movement

Rebels, revolutionaries, and those tired of being the elders' pawns.

- **Ideology**: Freedom from the tyranny of the ancients. "Our right, our fight."
- **Structure**: Far more loose. Some cities have Barons, but more often power is split among gangs and coteries.
- **Style**: Live closer to humans, make active use of modern technology and subcultures.
- **Today**: Currently on a strong upswing. Have taken advantage of the Camarilla's weakening and the elders' departure.

#### Autarkis (the Independents)

Vampires who refuse to swear allegiance to any sect.

- **Ideology**: "I belong to myself alone." May be ancient hermits or young Kindred who simply want to live their unlife.
- **Structure**: None. Every Autarkis is their own law within their own small domain.
- **Examples**: The Old World vors of Little Odessa, ancient monsters in the mountains, or loners in ruined cities.

#### The Sabbat

The most savage faction, and the most alien to humanity. They worship the Beast and the Dark Father.

- **Ideology**: The vampire is the top of the food chain. Humanity is weakness.
- **Structure**: Militarized packs, bound by ritual and harsh discipline.
- **Today**: Badly weakened after wars with the Camarilla and the Second Inquisition. Now operate as guerrilla terrorists, striking sudden, savage blows.

### The Six Traditions of the Camarilla

The **Traditions** are ancient laws considered the foundation of vampire society. Though created by the Camarilla, they are recognized (at least nominally) by nearly all Kindred. Breaking a Tradition is one of the gravest crimes in the world of the night.

#### I. The Masquerade

*Let your kind be hidden from all who are not Kindred to you.*

**The gist:** Conceal your existence from mortals.  
**Violation:** Any act that might reveal the nature of vampires (public use of Disciplines, leaving a body behind, video footage online, and so on).

**Sect attitudes:**
- **Camarilla** — absolute law. Violation is almost always punished by death.
- **Anarchs** — observe it, but more flexibly.
- **Sabbat** — often ignore it, considering it a weakness.

#### II. Domain

*Let your domain remain in your absolute power.*

**The gist:** Every Prince (or Baron) is the sovereign master of their city or territory. Hunting on another's land without permission is a grave insult.

**Sect attitudes:**
- **Camarilla** — strictly observed.
- **Anarchs** — recognize it, but often contest it by force.
- **Autarkis** — ignore it entirely.

#### III. Progeny

*Let your child receive the Embrace only by the word of your Elder.*

**The gist:** New vampires may be created only with the Prince's permission.

**Sect attitudes:**
- **Camarilla** — a strict rule.
- **Anarchs** — often broken.
- **Sabbat** — sire progeny en masse.

#### IV. Accounting

*Let the sire be the master of their child, until the child knows their place.*

**The gist:** A sire is responsible for their childer's actions until the childer is formally presented to the court.

#### V. Hospitality

*Honor the domain of your fellow Kindred. Let every Kindred who comes to a city present themselves before the face of its ruler.*

**The gist:** Every visiting Kindred is obliged to present themselves to the domain's ruler.

#### VI. Destruction

*Let no Kindred die by your hand. Let their life and death rest in the hands of your Elders.*

**The gist:** Only the Prince (or Baron) may kill another vampire. Violation results in the **Blood Hunt** (Lextalionis) — anyone may kill the offender without consequence.

---

**A Note for the Storyteller:**  
The Traditions are not merely rules. They are a tool of politics. The Camarilla uses them for control, the Anarchs as grounds for rebellion, while the Sabbat regards most of them as a sign of weakness. Use the breaking of a Tradition as a powerful story hook.

### The Second Inquisition

Today, the chief threat to the Kindred comes not from some mythical Gehenna, nor from their own kind, but from ordinary mortals. The Vatican, and the intelligence services of the United States and Great Britain (later joined by other countries), have united into a secret alliance known as the **Second Inquisition**.

They call vampires **"Blankbodies"** or "hemophages" — cold objects that give off no heat signature on night-vision equipment.

#### Key Strikes by the Second Inquisition

- **2008** — destruction of the main Tremere Chantry in Vienna. Officially, a terrorist attack.
- The elimination of the Princes of London and Las Vegas.
- Large-scale operations clearing out entire domains, using drones, special forces, and modern surveillance equipment.

#### Methods of Operation

- Global digital surveillance (PRISM and similar programs).
- Use of thermal imaging, satellites, and AI to hunt for "Blankbodies."
- Hunting ghouls and agents of influence.
- Torture and interrogation aimed at exposing other Kindred.

**To the Kindred,** the Inquisitors are no heroes. They are unscrupulous professionals, prepared to use any means: torture, blackmail, extrajudicial killing.

### The Masquerade in the Digital Age

The **Masquerade** is no longer simply a tradition — it is a matter of physical survival.

In an age of smartphones, 360° cameras, neural networks, and constant online surveillance, hiding the existence of the undead has become almost impossible. A single video on TikTok or Instagram Reels can lead hunters straight to your haven.

#### How the Masquerade Has Changed

- The **Camarilla** has imposed a total digital ban: no smartphones for communication between Kindred, ShreckNET fully shut down. Important orders are passed only by word of mouth or by paper letter.
- The **Anarchs** use "burner" phones, encryption, and human intermediaries.
- **Autarkis and Thin-bloods** have an advantage — they can lead relatively normal digital lives.
- The most effective way to remove a rival is to anonymously "tip off" the Second Inquisition to their location.

**The rule of survival in the 2020s:**  
If you've been caught on camera or online, you are already potentially dead. The best Kindred today are the ones no one notices at all.

### Brujah

**Brief Description of the Brujah**  
The Brujah are the clan of passion, rebellion, and untamable fury. Once philosopher-kings of ancient Carthage, today they form the heart of the Anarch Movement. The Brujah are those who cannot stay silent in the face of injustice. They are at once the most idealistic and the most dangerous of Kindred: capable of sparking a revolution in a single night, or of burning everything to the ground in a fit of rage.

#### Brujah Archetypes

- **Voice of the People / Activist** — a street agitator, feminist, environmentalist, leftist revolutionary. Willing to risk their unlife for an idea.
- **Philosopher-Fighter** — an intellectual with a heavy fist. Remembers when the Brujah ruled cities, and still considers their clan "the true aristocracy of the spirit."
- **Rocker / Troll** — a punk, metalhead, provocateur. What matters to them is not so much the meaning of the protest as the act of tearing down the old world.
- **Wolf in Sheep's Clothing** — outwardly an ordinary person (teacher, bartender, family man) desperately trying to hold on to the remnants of their humanity, but who could snap at any moment.
- **Old Guard** — a veteran of past wars, tired of ideals but who still hates the elders with all their heart.

#### Brujah Disciplines

- **Celerity** — supernatural speed. Allows movement and strikes faster than the eye can follow.
- **Potence** — raw physical strength. The Brujah can smash through walls, lift cars, and tear opponents apart with their bare hands.
- **Presence** — power over emotion. They can inspire a crowd, instill terror, or make people follow them through charisma alone.

#### Bane: Violent Blood

The Brujah's blood literally boils. They are far more susceptible to **Frenzy** than other clans.

**How it works:**  
Any serious provocation, insult, or stress sharply raises the chance of slipping into frenzy. The Bane's Severity directly increases the difficulty of resisting Frenzy. In the grip of frenzy, a Brujah becomes incredibly dangerous, but loses control of themselves and may attack allies or break the Masquerade in the crudest possible way.

**Examples:**
- A Brujah politician is insulted by the Prince at Elysium → instead of replying, they wreck half the hall.
- An Anarch Brujah watches the Inquisition kill their mortal friend → flies into a frenzy and stages a bloody massacre on camera.
- Even a simple failure at something important can trigger the "red haze."

### Ventrue

**Brief Description of the Ventrue**  
The Ventrue are the blue-blood clan, born rulers and the elite. They consider themselves the natural leaders of Kindred society and shepherds of humanity. Disciplined, ambitious, and supremely arrogant, the Ventrue are better than anyone at keeping up appearances and ruling others. They form the backbone of the Camarilla and most often occupy the thrones of Princes.

#### Ventrue Archetypes

- **Corporate Magnate** — CEO, banker, holding-company owner. Runs mortal business empires by day and a domain by night.
- **Old Aristocrat** — descendant of European noble houses, connoisseur of tradition, wine, and etiquette.
- **Politician / Senator** — master of backroom dealing, a deputy, lobbyist, or shadow puppeteer.
- **Crime Boss** — a "godfather," head of a mafia family who rules the streets with an iron hand.
- **Modern Prince** — a young (by Ventrue standards) ambitious neonate who wants to reform the Camarilla for modern times.

#### Ventrue Disciplines

- **Dominate** — absolute control over the mind. Allows them to give orders, erase memories, and rewrite another's will.
- **Fortitude** — supernatural endurance and resilience. Makes the Ventrue incredibly hard to kill.
- **Presence** — charisma and a commanding voice. Makes those around them respect, fear, or bow before them.

#### Bane: Discerning Taste

The Ventrue are extremely picky about the blood they drink. Each Ventrue can only feed on **one specific type of vessel**, matching their own personal criteria.

**How it works:**  
During character creation, the player and the Storyteller together determine this "taste" (for example: only successful businesspeople, only blondes, only people with a higher education, only military personnel, only artists, and so on). Blood of any other type barely sates the Hunger and causes strong revulsion. The higher the generation and the more severe the Bane, the narrower and stricter the restriction becomes.

**Examples:**
- A Ventrue CEO can drink only the blood of top executives and business owners.
- A Ventrue aristocrat — only the blood of people of noble birth, or "pure" blood.
- A young Ventrue hacker — only the blood of people with a technical mindset.

If a Ventrue is forced to feed on "unsuitable" blood, they gain less satiation and risk slipping into a mild frenzy out of revulsion.

### Gangrel

**Brief Description of the Gangrel**  
The Gangrel are the clan of wild predators, nomads, and those who have taken the Beast closest to heart. They reject cities, politics, and the conventions of civilization. Gangrel value only strength, freedom, and the ability to survive. Many of them have left the Camarilla and joined the Anarchs, or remained Autarkis.

#### Gangrel Archetypes

- **Lone Wolf** — a wanderer who roams the country, avoiding large cities and others' domains.
- **Urban Stalker** — a city hunter who knows every alley and sewer of their district better than the Prince does.
- **Former Convict / Survivalist** — someone Embraced after a hard life, who has now embraced the nature of a predator even more fully.
- **Barbarian Elder** — an ancient Gangrel who remembers when the clan lived in forests and steppes.
- **Pack Protector** — the leader of a small group of Gangrel or a coterie, who fiercely guards their territory and "their own."

#### Gangrel Disciplines

- **Animalism** — full understanding and control over animals. Allows them to speak with animals, summon packs, and see through their eyes.
- **Fortitude** — incredible resilience. Gangrel can withstand monstrous damage and keep fighting.
- **Protean** — power over their own body. Growing claws, transforming into animals, merging with the earth, and even turning to mist at high levels.

#### Bane: Bestial Features

When a Gangrel slips into **Frenzy** or spends Blood heavily, their body begins to display animal features that remain visible for a long while afterward.

**How it works:**  
After every fit of frenzy or critical expenditure of Blood, the Gangrel gains one or more **bestial features** (fur on the arms, feline eyes, claws that won't retract, an animal scent, scales, fangs, and so on). These features carry a social penalty (especially when dealing with civilized Kindred) and can expose the vampire's nature. The features fade only gradually, after a long rest or by spending Willpower.

**Examples:**
- After a fight with the Inquisition, a Gangrel is left with wolf ears and fur on their face — they have to hide in the sewers for several nights.
- A Gangrel in frenzy grows enormous claws that don't disappear by the end of the night — there's no way to show up at Elysium looking normal.
- Steadily accumulating features can make a Gangrel look like an actual beast, badly complicating the Masquerade.

### Malkavians

**Brief Description of the Malkavians**  
The Malkavians are the clan of mad visionaries, those who see the world "too clearly." Their minds are broken, but it is precisely through these cracks that they receive visions, hidden connections, and incredible intuition. To other Kindred, they are unpredictable psychopaths best left untouched. To themselves, they are the ones who understand the true nature of reality.

#### Malkavian Archetypes

- **Prophet / Visionary** — a mystic, medium, or philosopher who receives genuine insight through madness.
- **Manipulative Troll** — a master of psychological games who can break a person (or a vampire) with a single conversation.
- **Genius Psychopath** — a brilliant scientist, hacker, or strategist whose madness reveals solutions a sane mind cannot reach.
- **Jester / Clown** — at first glance a harmless eccentric, who in truth pulls the strings of the entire domain.
- **Traumatized Oracle** — someone Embraced after severe psychological trauma. They have now turned their pain into a weapon.

#### Malkavian Disciplines

- **Auspex** — heightened senses and the reading of auras. Allows them to see what is hidden, perceive emotions, and even glimpse fragments of the future.
- **Dominate** — control over the mind. Malkavians especially love passing on their madness and psychoses to their victims.
- **Obfuscate** — the ability to become unnoticed, by manipulating the perceptions of those around them.

#### Bane: Incurable Psychosis

Every Malkavian suffers from at least one serious mental disorder that cannot be cured.

**How it works:**  
The Bane is always present, but flares especially strongly in moments of stress, failure, or whenever the Storyteller judges it dramatically fitting. The psychosis imposes penalties on rolls, compels strange behavior, and can lead to dangerous complications (such as breaking the Masquerade). The Bane's Severity determines how strongly and how often the psychosis affects the character.

**Examples:**
- **Paranoia** — convinced everyone around them is conspiring against them (even allies).
- **Multiple Personality** — periodically "switches" to other personalities with different voices and mannerisms.
- **Hyperempathy** — feels everyone's emotions around them so strongly that they may lose control.
- **Pyromania/Counting Mania** — an obsession with fire, or with counting objects, during an episode.
- **Prophetic Hallucinations** — sees signs and messages everywhere, even where none exist.

### Nosferatu

**Brief Description of the Nosferatu**  
The Nosferatu are the clan of monstrous outcasts, kings of the sewers and lords of information. Their curse manifests immediately after the Embrace: they become extremely hideous. But it is this very hideousness that makes them strong — they see the world as it truly is, without illusions. The Nosferatu are the Kindred's finest spies, informants, and "rats."

#### Nosferatu Archetypes

- **King of the Sewers** — ruler of the underground world, who knows all the city's secrets.
- **Information Broker** — one who buys and sells secrets. The domain's chief source of gossip.
- **Street Detective** — an investigator who lives in the shadows and is always one step ahead of everyone.
- **Shrek** — a monster fully resigned to their hideousness, who uses fear as a weapon.
- **Shadow Enforcer** — muscle for the Camarilla or the Anarchs, who solves problems through "dirty" methods.

#### Nosferatu Disciplines

- **Obfuscate** — mastery of hiding and becoming unnoticed. Allows them to literally vanish from sight.
- **Animalism** — control over animals, especially rats and insects. Ideal for surveillance and attacks.
- **Potence** — raw physical strength. The Nosferatu can be unexpectedly dangerous in close combat.

#### Bane: Repulsiveness

The Nosferatu acquire an extremely repulsive appearance immediately after the Embrace — ranging from mild deformity to true monstrosity.

**How it works:**  
They automatically suffer a heavy penalty on social rolls tied to appearance (-2 or worse). Ordinary people feel disgust and fear in their presence. Even other Kindred often treat them with revulsion. Attempts to hide the deformity using Disciplines (such as Mask of a Thousand Faces) work less well for them than for others.

**Examples:**
- A Nosferatu enters Elysium — everyone present can't help recoiling and grimacing.
- An attempt to seduce or persuade a mortal triggers a harsh penalty, even when using Presence.
- In the modern world, the deformity is especially dangerous: cameras and smartphones easily capture their true appearance.

### Toreador

**Brief Description of the Toreador**  
The Toreador are the clan of artists, hedonists, and connoisseurs of beauty. They consider themselves the soul and heart of the Camarilla. For them, art, passion, and aesthetics are the only things separating them from the Beast. The Toreador are at once the most refined and the most obsessive of Kindred: capable of creating a masterpiece, or of ruining someone's life over a single "insufficiently beautiful" moment.

#### Toreador Archetypes

- **True Artist** — a painter, musician, writer, or director, for whom art matters more than unlife itself.
- **Socialite / Impresario** — master of the nightlife, owner of clubs and galleries, a trendsetter.
- **Patron** — a talent hunter who "sponsors" mortal geniuses, then turns them into their own lambs.
- **Aesthete Maniac** — someone obsessed with perfection in everything, from clothing to conversation partners.
- **Fallen Idealist** — a Toreador who once believed in the beauty of the world, and is now slowly going mad at its ugliness.

#### Toreador Disciplines

- **Presence** — absolute power over emotion. They can charm, instill love, or terror with a single smile.
- **Auspex** — heightened senses. Allows them to see the smallest details of beauty, and weaknesses in others' auras.
- **Celerity** — supernatural grace and speed. Makes a Toreador's movements beautiful even in combat.

#### Bane: Aesthetic Fixation

The Toreador are literally incapable of ignoring beauty (or its absence).

**How it works:**  
When a Toreador encounters something truly beautiful (or truly hideous), they may fall into a trance — literally freezing in place to admire it, forgetting all danger. They also suffer penalties and irritation in "ugly" surroundings. The higher the Bane's Severity, the stronger and longer the trance lasts.

**Examples:**
- A Toreador sees a perfect painting or an unbelievably beautiful person → freezes like a statue in the middle of a firefight.
- In a filthy, abandoned factory, they feel physically ill and struggle to concentrate.
- May skip an important meeting simply because "the office decor is disgusting."

### Tremere

**Brief Description of the Tremere**  
The Tremere are the clan of blood sorcerers, scholars, and manipulators. Once mortal mages, they stole immortality from vampires by force. Today they are one of the most enigmatic and dangerous clans. After the destruction of their main stronghold in Vienna, the Tremere fractured, but never lost their thirst for knowledge and power. They still believe that blood magic is the key to true dominion.

#### Tremere Archetypes

- **Classic Sorcerer** — a strict occultist in robes, devoted to old rituals and hierarchy.
- **Modern Magnate** — a corporate sorcerer who uses laboratories, AI, and financial flows for magical experiments.
- **Reformer / Witch** — a member of House Carna, who has rejected the old male-dominated hierarchy and seeks new paths of Blood.
- **Occult Consultant** — a hired specialist who sells their services to Princes and Barons.
- **Dangerous Experimenter** — a mad scientist willing to break any ethical rule for a breakthrough in magic.

#### Tremere Disciplines

- **Blood Sorcery** — unique blood magic. Allows them to craft rituals, turn blood into a weapon, summon fire, and lay powerful curses.
- **Auspex** — heightened perception and the reading of auras. The Tremere use it to find magical traces and an opponent's weaknesses.
- **Dominate** — control over the mind. Especially effective combined with rituals for erasing memories and conducting interrogations.

#### Bane: Deficient Blood

After the events of 2008 and internal upheavals, Tremere blood has become "tainted."

**How it works:**  
The Tremere are **almost unable** to form Blood Bonds in other Kindred (extremely high difficulty, bordering on impossible). At the same time, they themselves remain vulnerable to Bonds from other vampires. They also need considerably more blood to create or sustain ghouls. Many Tremere see this as a terrible weakening of the clan.

**Examples:**
- A Tremere tries to bind a powerful ally to themselves — but no Bond forms.
- Creating a ghoul costs them two or three times more vitae than it would any other Kindred.
- A Tremere can easily fall under the Bond of a stronger vampire themselves, which makes them paranoid about questions of trust.

### Caitiff

**Brief Description of the Caitiff**  
The Caitiff are vampires whose blood "took" no clan at all. They carry no strong curse and none of an Antediluvian's characteristic traits. Kindred society regards them as trash, "street urchins," or a potential threat. Yet it is precisely this freedom from clan restrictions that makes them some of the most flexible and unpredictable vampires of the modern age.

#### Caitiff Archetypes

- **Street Urchin / Survivalist** — a childer abandoned or orphaned by their sire right after the Embrace. Survived on their own and now trusts no one.
- **Secret Impostor** — a Caitiff who successfully passes themselves off as a member of another clan (most often Ventrue or Toreador), hiding their true origin.
- **Ideological Rebel** — someone who deliberately rejects clan identity and takes pride in their status as "free blood."
- **Victim of Experimentation** — a Caitiff created by a failed Tremere ritual or other magical mishap.
- **Lone Avenger** — someone Embraced in haste during a war or the Second Inquisition, now simply trying to survive in this new world.

#### Caitiff Disciplines

- **Total Freedom of Choice** — at character creation, the player chooses any three Disciplines (even ones that don't normally combine).
- **Flexible Learning** — Caitiff find it slightly easier to pick up out-of-clan Disciplines (only needing to taste the teacher's blood once).
- **No Clan Bonuses** — there are no "in-clan" Disciplines, so advancement in any of them follows the standard (more expensive) cost.

#### Bane: Social Stigma

The Caitiff carry no mystical curse, but suffer from harsh social rejection.

**How it works:**  
Any Kindred who knows they are dealing with a Caitiff treats them with prejudice and contempt. This imposes an automatic penalty on social rolls (usually -1 or -2 dice, and up to -3 at a high Bane Severity). In the Camarilla, Caitiff are often used as cannon fodder or scapegoats. Many Princes outright forbid them from being in the city without special permission.

**Examples:**
- A Caitiff comes to Elysium — the Primogen and Harpies immediately start whispering and demand they be "kept away from important matters."
- An attempt to form an alliance or gain status almost always comes with a serious penalty.
- In extreme cases, a Caitiff may be declared "kinless" and handed a Blood Hunt simply for "belonging to no one."

### Thin-bloods

**Brief Description of the Thin-bloods**  
The Thin-bloods are 14th, 15th, and sometimes 16th generation vampires. Their blood is so diluted that they barely feel Caine's ancient curse at all. To most full-blooded Kindred, they are a disgrace and "tainted blood," but it is precisely the thin-bloods who are best adapted to the modern world and the Second Inquisition. They are living proof that the curse weakens with every generation.

#### Thin-blood Archetypes

- **Everyman** — someone trying to hold on to a normal life: a job, a family, a mortgage. Now works by day and hunts by night.
- **Soldier of Fortune** — a childer Embraced in haste during a war or conflict. Often used as cannon fodder by coteries.
- **Repentant Sinner** — someone who considers vampirism a disease and seeks a way to cure it, or atone for their guilt.
- **Digital Nomad** — a hacker, streamer, or blogger who uses their nearly-human abilities to survive in the modern world.
- **Avenger** — a thin-blood who watched their sire or coterie be destroyed, and now seeks revenge.

#### Thin-blood Disciplines

- **Thin-Blood Alchemy** — their unique "discipline." Allows them to craft powerful potions and concoctions from blood, Resonance, and chemicals, mimicking the effects of other Disciplines.
- **Temporary Powers** — by drinking blood with a particular **Resonance**, they can temporarily gain access to the powers of almost any Discipline.
- **Hybrid Potential** — can combine weak versions of different Disciplines, granting unusual tactical options.

#### Bane: Diluted Blood

The Thin-bloods carry no classic, heavy clan curse, but their blood is extremely weak.

**How it works:**  
- Blood Potency is always **0** — they can barely enhance their traits through vitae.
- They gain fewer benefits from feeding (the Hunger is sated less effectively).
- Many powerful effects from older generations work weaker on them, or not at all.
- In exchange, they have **significant advantages** over normal vampires: they can walk in the sun (with protection), eat normal food, and suffer less from fire and a stake through the heart.

**Examples:**
- A thin-blood can go outside by day in heavy clothing, sunglasses, and sunscreen, and not burn within minutes.
- An ordinary stake through the heart is, for them, just a serious wound rather than instant torpor.
- When trying to use the powerful Disciplines of older vampires, the effect is considerably weaker or requires extra cost.

### Hecata (House of Hecata)

**Brief Description of the Hecata**  
The Hecata are the heirs of the old Cappadocian and Giovanni clans. They are necromancers, keepers of the secrets of death, and those who deal with ghosts. After the fall of the old Cappadocians and the breakup of the Giovanni, they united into the House of Hecata. To them, death is not an end but a door — one that can be opened, studied, and used. Kindred society respects them, fears them, and often dislikes them.

#### Hecata Archetypes

- **Soul Thief** — a necromancer who collects ghosts and uses them as spies and weapons.
- **Family Cultist** — a member of a large family dynasty in which necromancy is passed down through generations.
- **Grave Keeper** — the caretaker of ancient cemeteries and mausoleums, who knows all the secrets of the dead.
- **Corporate Medium** — works with wealthy mortals, helping them "communicate" with deceased relatives (for very large sums of money).
- **Dark Exorcist** — a specialist in banishing and binding dangerous ghosts.

#### Hecata Disciplines

- **Auspex** — heightened senses and the reading of auras. Helps them see ghosts and the remnants of life in the dead.
- **Fortitude** — supernatural endurance. The Hecata are very hard to kill permanently.
- **Oblivion** — necromancy and power over the shadows of death. Allows them to summon ghosts, manipulate shadow, and drain life force.

#### Bane: Curse of the Grave

The Hecata bear the mark of death more strongly than other vampires.

**How it works:**  
A faint but unmistakable smell of grave earth and decay constantly clings to them. Living people in their presence often feel an inexplicable fear or nausea. Ghosts and spirits are drawn to them more strongly, sometimes becoming intrusive or dangerous. At high Bane Severity, the smell grows stronger, and contact with the living becomes even more unpleasant.

**Examples:**
- A Hecata enters a room — ordinary people can't help wrinkling their noses and wanting to leave.
- Ghosts of the dead regularly appear nearby, demanding attention or help.
- In moments of deep hunger, the scent of death grows so heavy that it's hard to conceal even with Disciplines.

### The Ministry (Setites)

**Brief Description of the Ministry**  
The Ministry is the modern name of the Setite clan. Serpents, seducers, prophets of false gods, and masters of cultural decay. They are not merely vampires — they are ideologues and cultists who believe true freedom comes through tearing down the old order and embracing the Beast. Masters of manipulating emotion, religion, and desire.

#### Ministry Archetypes

- **Prophet of False Gods** — a charismatic cult leader who builds new religions around themselves.
- **Seductive Politician** — a master of seduction and blackmail, operating in the upper echelons of power.
- **Cultist of Ruin** — someone who infiltrates organizations and slowly rots them from within.
- **Seductress / Tempter** — the classic serpent archetype, breaking will through passion and dependency.
- **Modern Guru** — a blogger, coach, or influencer who sells "enlightenment" laced with poison.

#### Ministry Disciplines

- **Presence** — absolute power over emotion and desire. The finest seducers of any clan.
- **Obfuscate** — mastery of hiding and deceiving perception. Ideal for infiltration and disappearance.
- **Dominate** — control over the mind. Especially effective combined with Presence to create fanatical followers.

#### Bane: Flaw of Faith

Every Setite is bound to some deep, often perverse belief or philosophy that they cannot violate without severe consequences.

**How it works:**  
Every member of the Ministry has a **personal Code** or prohibition (for example: "never lie to my followers," "always keep my word," "never refuse pleasure," and so on). Breaking this Code triggers a powerful pang of remorse, a loss of Willpower, and can provoke Frenzy. The higher the Bane's Severity, the stricter and more dangerous the prohibition.

**Examples:**
- A Setite who believes in "freedom through pleasure" cannot turn down an offered pleasure, even if it's a trap.
- A prophet who swore "never to betray my followers" is physically unable to hand them over to the Inquisition.
- Breaking their faith causes heavy moral damage and a long recovery of Willpower.

### Lasombra

**Brief Description of the Lasombra**  
The Lasombra are the clan of shadows, inquisitors, and lords of darkness. They have always been one of the most ruthless and disciplined powers within the Camarilla. Masters of manipulating fear, shadow, and power. Even after the schism, many Lasombra remain loyal to the old traditions and consider themselves the true keepers of order among the Kindred.

#### Lasombra Archetypes

- **Inquisitor** — a merciless judge and executioner who purges "heresy" within the sect.
- **Shadow Manipulator** — a master of backroom intrigue who rules while staying in the shadows.
- **Church Politician** — a vampire who works through religious structures, or plays the role of a "dark cardinal."
- **Knight of Shadows** — a warrior combining harsh discipline with power over darkness.
- **Fallen Idealist** — a Lasombra who once believed in the clan's nobility, and is now disillusioned and cynical.

#### Lasombra Disciplines

- **Dominate** — absolute control of the mind. The Lasombra use it to create total submission.
- **Obfuscate** — mastery of hiding in shadow and deceiving perception.
- **Fortitude** — supernatural endurance. Makes them very hard to kill in combat.

#### Bane: Curse of Shadow

The Lasombra suffer more than others from light and fire.

**How it works:**  
Sunlight and fire deal them **additional heavy damage**. They also suffer penalties when trying to use Obfuscate or other powers in brightly lit places. At high Bane Severity, even ordinary bright light can cause significant discomfort and weakness.

**Examples:**
- A Lasombra caught in an Inquisition searchlight's beam suffers far more pain and damage than an ordinary vampire would.
- Using Obfuscate in a well-lit room weakens the effect, or removes it entirely.
- Fire is, for them, one of the most terrifying weapons that exists.

### Tzimisce

**Brief Description of the Tzimisce**  
The Tzimisce are an ancient and terrible clan of flesh-lords. Masters of biological horror, sculptors of living matter, and sadistic aesthetes. They see the body as clay, to be reshaped however they please. Very few remain in the modern world, but those who have survived still inspire primal fear even in other elders.

#### Tzimisce Archetypes

- **Lord of Flesh** — a sadistic surgeon who creates "works of art" out of living creatures.
- **Ancient Feudal Lord** — an old warlord who rules their territory like a medieval tyrant.
- **Biological Experimenter** — a mad scientist conducting monstrous experiments on flesh.
- **Maker of Monsters** — creates ghouls, zombie-like creatures, and terrible guardians.
- **Fallen Aristocrat** — a refined monster who considers everyone else an "unfinished work."

#### Tzimisce Disciplines

- **Protean** — supreme power over one's own body and the bodies of others. Allows them to mold flesh like clay.
- **Blood Sorcery** — potent blood magic, especially strong in rituals tied to flesh and transformation.
- **Fortitude** — incredible resilience. The Tzimisce are very hard to kill permanently.

#### Bane: Flaw of Flesh

A Tzimisce's body constantly demands attention and "maintenance," or else it begins to deform and rot.

**How it works:**  
The Tzimisce must regularly spend blood and time keeping their body in "acceptable" condition. If they neglect this, hideous growths, open wounds, displaced organs, and other horrors appear. At a high Bane Severity, the deformities happen faster and demand more resources. Many Tzimisce wear masks or keep their bodies fully hidden beneath clothing.

**Examples:**
- After a long fight or a period of hunger, bony growths and exposed flesh appear on a Tzimisce's face.
- To look "normal" before appearing at Elysium, they spend a significant amount of blood and time on rituals.
- In a neglected state, they can turn into a true monster, one hard to hide even with Disciplines.

### Ravnos

**Brief Description of the Ravnos**  
The Ravnos are the clan of wandering tricksters, illusionists, and keepers of secrets. They are often called "gypsies" or "liars." Masters of deception, illusion, and survival on the move. They never linger long in one place and always keep several "backup" stories ready. In the modern world, the Ravnos are among the most adaptable of vampires.

#### Ravnos Archetypes

- **Traveling Performer** — a musician, magician, or street performer who moves from city to city.
- **High-Class Con Artist** — a swindler, card cheat, master of social engineering.
- **Keeper of Secrets** — someone who collects and sells secrets, legends, and forbidden knowledge.
- **Nomadic Survivalist** — a Ravnos who lives on the road and never trusts settled Kindred.
- **Trickster** — a jester and provocateur who wrecks others' plans simply because they can.

#### Ravnos Disciplines

- **Obfuscate** — masters of illusion and deceiving perception. Can craft elaborate hallucinations.
- **Auspex** — heightened senses and intuition. Helps them sense danger ahead of time and read people.
- **Celerity** — supernatural speed and grace. Ideal for escape and quick tricks.

#### Bane: Curse of Deceit

The Ravnos are physically and mentally unable to stay honest for long.

**How it works:**  
They are compelled to constantly lie, play roles, or mislead someone. If a Ravnos speaks nothing but the truth, or leads an "honest" life, for too long, they begin to suffer from severe discomfort, a loss of Willpower, and may slip into Frenzy. The higher the Bane's Severity, the more often and more heavily their curse must be "fed" with deception.

**Examples:**
- A Ravnos cannot simply answer a question straight — they will inevitably add a lie or a half-truth.
- If they try to run an honest business or relationship, they quickly lose control and snap.
- In extreme cases, they may start deceiving even themselves, leading to dangerous hallucinations.

### The Philosophy of Character Creation

In **Vampire: The Masquerade 5th Edition**, creating a character is not simply filling out a sheet. It is the birth of a tragic story about a person who died and is now forced to exist inside a monster's body.

It's important to remember the main thing here:  
**You are not playing a superhero with super powers. You are playing a human being who was given immortality, and an eternal hunger to go with it.**

#### The Character Creation Triad

V5 is built around three inseparably linked elements:

- **The Character** — who you were in life, who you became after the Embrace, and who you want to (or are forced to) become next.
- **The Coterie** — your "family" by night. Most of the drama unfolds through relationships within the coterie.
- **The Chronicle** — the shared story you tell together. The character must fit organically into it.

If any one of these elements falters, the whole game suffers. That's why character creation always begins with a conversation at the table.

#### What Makes a Character Truly Interesting in V5

- **Inner conflict** — the remnants of humanity against the Hunger and the Beast.
- **Connection to the world** — Touchstones, Convictions, mortal loved ones.
- **The price of immortality** — what you've already lost, and what you're willing to lose next.
- **Vividness** — a memorable Predator Type, ambitions, and fears.

A good character in V5 is not the strongest or the "coolest." It's a character whose fall (or struggle to keep their humanity) is painfully interesting to watch.

#### Important Questions Before Creation

- What level of chronicle are you playing? (street-level survival, urban intrigue, or large-scale politics)
- How dark will the game be? (from "dark grey" to full-blown horror)
- Are there themes better avoided?
- Do you want to start the game as already-experienced neonates, or as freshly Embraced childer?

**Remember:**  
Your character doesn't have to be "good." They have to be **interesting**. Even if they're a monster, it matters that you understand why they became one and what that means to them.

---

### Preparing for Creation

Before picking up a pencil and a character sheet, it's important to hold a **Session Zero**. This is one of the most important parts of the process in V5.

#### Session Zero — What to Discuss

- **Tone and level of the chronicle**  
  - Street-level (street survival, weaker characters)  
  - City-level (urban intrigue, mid-tier power)  
  - Domain-level (domain politics, more experienced characters)  
  - Chronicle-level (a grand story with room to grow into elder-hood)

- **Boundaries and safety at the table**  
  - Use of tools (X-Card, Stoplight, Veils, etc.)  
  - Off-limits topics (violence, sexual violence, specific phobias, etc.)

- **Player and Storyteller expectations**  
  - How much politics / combat / personal drama / horror there will be  
  - Whether the chronicle will be pessimistic or carry glimmers of hope

- **Building connections**  
  - Who already knows whom within the future coterie  
  - Shared enemies or goals

#### Character Power Level

V5 offers several starting power levels for characters. They determine how experienced and powerful your hero will be at the start of the chronicle.

| Level          | Starting XP | Blood Potency | Humanity | Extra | Recommendation |
|------------------|----------------|------------|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Childer**       | 0 XP           | 1          | 7            | —             | **Best choice for a first chronicle** |
| **Neonates**      | 15 XP          | 1          | 7            | —             | The classic starting point |
| **Ancillae**      | 35 XP          | +1         | -1           | +2 Merit dots<br>+2 Flaw dots | For more experienced groups |
| **Elders**   | 50+ XP         | 3+         | 4 or lower     | By agreement with the Storyteller | High power level |

### Advancement Cost (Character Progression)

| Trait                        | Experience Cost                  |
|---------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Attribute**              | New rating × 5                |
| **Skill**                       | New rating × 3                |
| **New Specialty**         | 3 experience                           |
| **In-Clan Discipline**         | New rating × 5                |
| **Out-of-Clan Discipline**        | New rating × 7                |
| **Discipline for a Caitiff**      | New rating × 6                |
| **Blood Sorcery Ritual**| Ritual level × 3               |
| **Thin-Blood Formula**           | Formula level × 3             |
| **Merit**                | 3 experience per dot           |
| **Blood Potency**                  | New rating × 10               |

**Important rules of advancement:**
- New trait ratings are bought **sequentially** (you can't raise an Attribute straight from 2 to 4 — you must buy 3 first).
- The Storyteller may award bonus experience for good play, for acting on an Ambition/Desire, and for significant events in the chronicle.

**Recommendation:**  
For a first chronicle, it's almost always best to start as a **Childer** or **Neonate** (0–15 XP). This lets players more deeply feel the horror of the Embrace, the pressure of the Hunger, and helplessness before the older generations.

### Step-by-Step Character Creation (10 Steps)

#### 1. Concept

This is the **most important** step in character creation. This is where, instead of a set of numbers on a sheet, a living, tragic hero with their own story is born.

Decide **who you were in life** and **who you became after the Embrace**. A good concept answers the central question: "Why will it be interesting to watch this character over the next several years of their unlife?"

**Questions to consider:**
- What did you do before death? (profession, social status, dreams, fears)
- How did your Embrace happen? (violent, willing, accidental, a betrayal, an act of love, revenge?)
- What is your main inner conflict right now? (what have you already lost, and what are you afraid of losing forever?)
- What do you want to achieve in your unlife? (your **Ambition**)
- What truly draws you in and distracts you? (your **Desire**)

##### Useful Tools for Developing Your Concept

Although V5 has **no** Nature and Demeanor mechanics, many players and Storytellers still use them as a powerful tool for understanding a character's psychology:

- **Archetype** — how others see you, in a single word (the role you play).
- **Nature** — your true nature, who you are at the deepest level.
- **Demeanor** — the mask you show the world.

**Example Concepts:**

1. **Gangrel**  
   - Archetype: Bruiser  
   - Nature: Strategist  
   - Demeanor: Trickster  
   → A cunning, calculating predator who plays the part of a reckless clown.

2. **Ventrue Neonate**  
   - Archetype: Crown Prince  
   - Nature: Judge  
   - Demeanor: Life of the Party  
   → Outwardly a charming playboy, inwardly a cold and principled judge.

3. **Toreador Artist**  
   - Archetype: Muse  
   - Nature: Perfectionist  
   - Demeanor: Fragile Artist  
   → Obsessed with beauty, and willing to destroy anything that strikes her as imperfect.

---

#### 2. Clan and Sire

Choose a **clan** (or play a **Caitiff** / **Thin-blood**). For a detailed description of all clans, their archetypes, Disciplines, and Banes, see the **Clans and Bloodlines** section.

Your **Sire** is one of the most important elements of your character in **Vampire: The Masquerade**.

**Why the Sire matters so much:**
- They are the one who gave you the **Embrace** — killed you and reborn you as Kindred.
- Under the traditions and laws of the Camarilla, your Sire bears **Accounting** for all your actions until you are formally presented to the Prince.
- Your Sire is your first, and often your strongest, bond in the world of night. They might be a mentor, a tyrant, a lover, a betrayer, or a ghost from the past.
- Your relationship with your Sire heavily shapes your **Humanity**, possible Blood Bonds, and future story hooks.

**Questions worth working through:**
- Who is your Sire? (name, clan, age, personality)
- How did your Embrace happen? (violent, willing, accidental, tragic)
- What is your relationship now? (love, hatred, duty, fear, complete absence of contact)

**Tip:** Even if your Sire is already dead or has vanished, this is still an excellent source of drama and motivation.

---

#### 3. Generation

Generation determines the strength of your blood (**Blood Potency**) and how deeply Caine's ancient curse runs in you.

**Standard options for character creation:**
- **13th generation** — the most common and recommended choice.
- **14th–15th generations** — **Thin-bloods**.

**Lower generations** (12th and below) make a character considerably more powerful, but cost extra resources and **require the Storyteller's explicit consent**.

| Generation     | Blood Potency | Cost at Creation          | Rarity in Chronicles      | Recommendation |
|---------------|---------------|---------------------------------|--------------------------|--------------|
| 15th–14th       | 0             | 0 dots                        | Common                  | For newcomers and street-level chronicles |
| **13th**      | 1             | **0 dots**                    | Standard                 | **Recommended** |
| 12th          | 2             | +2 Merit dots           | Uncommon                 | Experienced groups |
| 11th          | 3             | +4 Merit dots           | Rare                     | Only with the Storyteller's consent |
| 10th          | 4             | +7 Merit dots           | Very rare             | Almost never |
| 9th and below    | 5+            | Storyteller's permission only    | Extremely rare      | For special NPCs only |

**Note:**  
Lowering your generation **does not** grant extra points for Attributes or Skills. It only raises Blood Potency and the abilities tied to it (more blood per turn, stronger Disciplines, and so on). For a first chronicle, it's almost always best to start at 13th generation or as a Thin-blood.

#### 4. Predator Type

Your **Predator Type** is your primary strategy and habit for obtaining blood. It heavily shapes how you exist in the world, which skills you develop, and what problems arise for you.

Choosing a Predator Type grants you:
- A free specialty
- A bonus to one Discipline (+1)
- Additional merits and/or flaws

---

##### Available Predator Types

###### Blood Leech  
You feed on the blood of other Kindred. This is extremely dangerous and almost always forbidden.  
- **Specialty:** Brawl (Kindred) or Stealth (against Kindred)  
- +1 to **Celerity** or **Protean**  
- -1 Humanity  
- +1 Blood Potency  
- Gain serious flaws (Disliked or Shunned)

###### Consensualist 
You never drink blood without the victim's consent.  
- **Specialty:** Medicine (phlebotomy) or Persuasion (vessels)  
- +1 to **Auspex** or **Fortitude**  
- +1 Humanity  
- Gain the "Prey Exclusion (Nonconsenting)" flaw
- Gain the "Dark Secret" flaw

###### Osiris 
You are an idol or a cult leader. You feed on the blood of your worshippers.  
- **Specialty:** Occult or Performance (choose a field)  
- +1 to **Presence** or **Blood Sorcery** (Tremere)  
- +3 dots split between "Fame" and "Herd"  
- Gain enemies and/or folkloric flaws

###### Siren
You feed during intimacy or its imitation.  
- **Specialty:** Persuasion (seduction) or Subterfuge (seduction)  
- +1 to **Fortitude** or **Presence**  
- The "Beautiful" merit (••)  
- Gain an enemy (a spurned lover)

###### Sandman  
You break into homes and feed on people while they sleep.  
- **Specialty:** Medicine (anesthesia) or Stealth (infiltration)  
- +1 to **Auspex** or **Obfuscate**  
- +1 Resources

###### Alleycat
You attack people by force and drink the blood of the defeated.  
- **Specialty:** Intimidation (mugging) or Brawl (grappling)  
- +1 to **Celerity** or **Potence**  
- -1 Humanity  
- +3 Contacts (criminal circles)

###### Cleaver
You secretly feed on the blood of your own mortal family or close friends.  
- **Specialty:** Persuasion (gaslighting) or Subterfuge (excuses)  
- +1 to **Dominate** or **Animalism**  
- +2 Herd  
- Gain the "Dark Secret (Cleaver)" flaw

###### Bagger
You don't hunt living people at all — you rely on donated blood, hospitals, or the black market.  
- **Specialty:** Larceny (break-ins) or Streetwise (black market)  
- +1 to **Obfuscate** or **Blood Sorcery** (Tremere)  
- The "Iron Gullet" merit (•••)  
- Ventrue **cannot** choose this type

###### Scene Queen
You move within a particular subculture and feed on its members.  
- **Specialty:** Etiquette / Leadership / Streetwise (choose a subculture)  
- +1 to **Dominate** or **Potence**  
- +1 Fame  
- +1 Contacts

###### Farmer
You feed exclusively on animal blood.  
- **Specialty:** Animal Ken or Survival (hunting)  
- +1 to **Animalism** or **Protean**  
- +1 Humanity  
- Gain the "Farmer" flaw (••)  
- Ventrue and vampires with Blood Potency 3+ **cannot** choose this type

---

**Tip:**  
Predator Type isn't just a mechanic — it's an important part of a character's **roleplaying identity**. It determines how you interact with the world and what problems will come your way. Choose it deliberately — it heavily shapes the tone of the game.

#### 5. Attributes

Attributes describe your character's **innate** abilities — what they were born with, and what is hard to change. They are divided into three categories of 3 attributes each.

**V5** uses a fixed distribution scheme:

- **1 Attribute** gets **4 dots** (your standout strength).
- **3 Attributes** get **3 dots each** (a good level).
- **4 Attributes** get **2 dots each** (an average human level).
- **1 Attribute** gets **1 dot** (a clear weakness).

##### Attribute Descriptions

###### Physical
- **Strength** — raw physical power. Lifting heavy weights, landing powerful blows, grappling, breaking down doors.
- **Dexterity** — coordination, reaction speed, precision of movement. Shooting, hand-to-hand combat, driving, acrobatics, sleight of hand.
- **Stamina** — the ability to endure strain for long periods, to resist pain, illness, and fatigue. Determines how much you can take before needing rest.

###### Social
- **Charisma** — natural charm and the ability to be liked. Ease in conversation, inspiring others, leading "from the heart."
- **Manipulation** — the ability to persuade, deceive, pressure, and get your way. Intrigue, blackmail, bargaining, seduction.
- **Composure** — emotional stability and self-control. The ability to keep a straight face under pressure, resist provocation, and stay cool-headed.

###### Mental
- **Intelligence** — logical thinking, memory, the capacity to learn and analyze. Solving complex problems, knowledge of theory, research.
- **Wits** — quickness of thought, intuition, resourcefulness under stress. The ability to improvise, notice details, and get out of sudden trouble.
- **Resolve** — willpower, focus, and the ability to see things through. Resistance to psychological pressure, long-term motivation, steadfastness against temptation.

**Recommendation:**  
Don't make all your attributes even. A good character usually has 1–2 clearly pronounced strengths and at least one noticeable weakness. This makes them feel alive and interesting to roleplay.

**Example Distribution:**
- 4 — Dexterity (you're very graceful and precise in your movements)
- 3 — Charisma, Wits, Stamina
- 2 — Strength, Manipulation, Intelligence, Composure
- 1 — Resolve (you break easily under prolonged pressure)

**Note:**  
The 7/5/3 scheme from previous editions is officially **not used** in V5. If your group wants to play with it, that's a house rule, and it needs to be discussed with the Storyteller in advance.

#### 6. Skills + Specialties

Skills reflect your character's **acquired** knowledge and abilities — what they have learned over the course of their life and unlife.

#### Physical

- **Athletics** — Running, jumping, climbing, dodging, and throwing. Anything tied to bodily coordination and physical conditioning.
- **Brawl** — Unarmed close combat: fists, grapples, improvised body weapons.
- **Craft** — Creating, repairing, and modifying objects — from weapons to works of art.
- **Drive** — Handling a vehicle in difficult conditions, pursuits, getaways, basic repairs.
- **Firearms** — Handling firearms, aiming, maintenance, and identification.
- **Larceny** — Lockpicking, pickpocketing, bypassing security systems, sleight of hand.
- **Melee** — Close combat with edged or blunt weapons (swords, knives, clubs).
- **Stealth** — Moving unnoticed, disguise, vanishing from sight.
- **Survival** — Navigating the wilderness, finding shelter, tracking, surviving in extreme conditions.

#### Social

- **Animal Ken** — Understanding animal behavior, training, calming animals, and using them for your own ends.
- **Etiquette** — Knowledge of social norms, knowing how to behave in high society, on the street, or in criminal circles.
- **Insight** — Reading emotions, motives, and lies through body language, microexpressions, and tone of voice.
- **Intimidation** — Instilling fear, making threats, using authority or brute force to compel obedience.
- **Leadership** — Motivating a group, giving commands, inspiring followers.
- **Performance** — Performing: acting, singing, dancing, public speaking before an audience.
- **Persuasion** — Logical and emotional influence, to win others over to your point of view.
- **Streetwise** — Knowledge of the streets, the criminal underworld, and where to get information or goods.
- **Subterfuge** — Deception, manipulation, lying, and spotting someone else's deceit.

#### Mental

- **Academics** — Humanities, history, literature, philosophy.
- **Awareness** — Attentiveness to details in your surroundings, spotting threats and clues.
- **Finance** — Managing money, investments, laundering, understanding economics.
- **Investigation** — Gathering evidence, analysis, solving puzzles and crimes.
- **Medicine** — Diagnosis, treating wounds, knowledge of anatomy and disease.
- **Occult** — Knowledge of the supernatural, rituals, vampiric and other mystical lore.
- **Politics** — The inner workings of power, intrigue, bureaucracy (whether mortal or that of the Camarilla/Anarchs).
- **Science** — Physics, chemistry, biology, and other natural sciences.
- **Technology** — Computers, hacking, modern electronics and gadgets.

##### What a Skill Rating Means

| Rating | Name          | What it means in practice |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| **0**   | No Skill        | You barely know how to do this. Rolls usually fail. |
| **1**   | Novice           | Basic knowledge. You can perform simple tasks. |
| **2**   | Average   | The normal professional level of an ordinary person. |
| **3**   | Professional      | An experienced specialist. A good level. |
| **4**   | Expert           | One of the best in the city/region. |
| **5**   | Master            | World-class. A legend. (Almost unattainable at character creation) |

##### Distributing 13 Skill Dots

V5 offers three recommended templates:

- **Narrow Specialist**  
  1 skill at 4 + 3 skills at 3 + 3 skills at 2 + 3 skills at 1

- **Balanced Development** (recommended)  
  5 skills at 3 + 3 skills at 2

- **Jack of All Trades**  
  1 skill at 3 + 9 skills at 2 + 1 skill at 1

**Free distribution** is fully allowed. The main constraints are:
- A total of **13 dots**.
- A maximum of **4** dots in any one skill at creation.
- A 5th dot can only be earned later, through experience.

##### Specialties

A **Specialty** is a narrow area within a skill in which you are especially good.

- It grants **+1 die** to any roll where it applies.
- At character creation, you get **one free specialty** for every skill rated **3 dots or higher**.
- Additional specialties can be bought with Merit dots (1 dot = 1 specialty).

**Examples:**
- Firearms (Handguns)
- Occult (Tremere Rituals)
- Etiquette (Camarilla)

#### 7. Merits & Flaws

At character creation, you get **7 dots** to spend on **Merits**.  
You may take **Flaws** to gain extra dots, but **no more than +2** (for a maximum of 9 Merit dots total at creation).

##### MERITS

Beyond vampiric powers and human skills, a character may have, from the very start of the game, certain **merits** — anything from a knack for languages to a band of loyal ghouls. Like other traits, merit ratings are measured in dots, from one to five. If a character's merit rating is 0, that's perfectly normal and carries no consequences.

Merits rarely figure directly into rolls, though the Storyteller might suggest an **Intelligence + Linguistics** roll if the character wants to decipher an ancient papyrus, or **Subterfuge + Contacts** to spread a rumor about an enemy.

Merits are divided into:
- **Looks**,
- **Background Facts**,
- **Background Chapters**.

**Flaws** are their mirror image.

**Important rule:** a character does not have to raise a merit's rating sequentially. Having a third-dot merit does **not** automatically grant the effects of the first and second dots.

---

###### LOOKS

Looks are qualities inherent to the character themself (physical, mental, or social). As a rule, they remain unchanged over the course of the chronicle.

---

**Anachronism**
*Only for ancillae and older vampires.*

- **Flaw (••) Living Anachronism.** You either spent a long time in torpor, or for some other reason never managed to adapt to the modern world. Your **Technology** Skill is 0 and cannot be raised. The Storyteller may reduce the pool of any roll involving modern technology by 1d10.

- **Flaw (•) Retrograde.** You hold seriously outdated beliefs ("the Pope's word is law," "a woman is a delicate flower," and so on). They support your Humanity, but strike many as odious. Social roll pools are reduced by 1d10, except when the other party shares the same views.

**Looks**
Modifiers apply only when the vampire can be seen.

- **Flaw (••) Repulsive.** Pools for all social rolls affected by appearance are reduced by **2d10**.
- **Flaw (•) Ugly.** Pools are reduced by **1d10**.
- **•• Beautiful.** Pools are increased by **1d10**.
- **•••• Stunning.** Pools are increased by **2d10**.

**Feeding Habits**

- **Flaw (••) Organovore.** You can only sate the Hunger **by devouring human flesh and organs**. Resonance is only transmitted through the heart.

- **Bloodhound.** You can smell Resonance in blood without tasting it. Roll **Resolve + Awareness** (difficulty 3). Difficulty shifts based on distance and stocks on hand.

- **Iron Gullet.** Cold, spoiled blood, and even fractionated plasma, sate the Hunger, but **do not** carry Resonance. Ventrue cannot take this merit.

- **Polyglot.** Each dot adds one additional language (reading, writing, speaking). Your native language plus the chronicle's language are free.

- **Flaw (••) Illiterate.** The **Academics** and **Science** Skills cannot be raised above 1. You cannot take modern specialties.

**Blood Bonds**

The **Blood Bond** is one of the most powerful and dangerous mystical phenomena in the world of the Kindred. It is a supernatural, almost irresistible emotional and spiritual attachment that arises when one vampire regularly drinks another's blood.

In essence, it is **mystical slavery**. Whoever drinks another Kindred's blood three times becomes obsessed with them.

**How Bonds Form**

For a Bond to form, you must drink the blood of the same Kindred **three times** (on different nights, usually within the span of a year). Every additional drink strengthens the bond.

**Bond Levels** (1 through 6):

| Level | Strength of the Bond                  | What the thrall feels |
|---------|--------------------------|--------------------|
| **1**   | Mild attachment     | Fondness, a wish to please |
| **2**   | Strong attachment    | Deep fondness, hard to act against their will |
| **3**   | Full submission        | The thrall can barely refuse their regnant |
| **4–6** | Absolute slavery       | The thrall is willing to die or kill for their regnant |

**Resistance Mechanics**

To disobey a direct order from their regnant, the thrall undergoes a **contest**:
- **Resolve + Intelligence** (thrall) against **Bond Strength** (regnant).

The higher the Bond's level, the harder it is to resist.

**Merits and Flaws Tied to the Bond**

- **Flaw (••) Slave to the Bond**  
  The Bond forms after just **one** drink instead of three.

- **Flaw (•) Bond Addict**  
  The pool for rolls to resist your regnant's will is reduced by **1d10**.

- **Flaw (•) Stable Bond**  
  The Bond only weakens by one level after **three months** without fresh blood.

- **• Bond Resistance**  
  +1d10 to the resistance pool per dot (maximum +3).

- **•• Unstable Bond**  
  The Bond weakens **twice a month** (at the full moon and the new moon).

- **•••• Bond Immunity**  
  You **cannot** be bound by a Blood Bond at all.

**Important Rules**

- Bonds only work in one direction: the thrall is bound to the regnant, never the reverse.
- The same Kindred can be Bonded to several different Kindred at once.
- A Bond can be weakened or broken, but this is extremely difficult and requires a long stretch without the regnant's blood, plus active resistance.

**Roleplay Example**

Your character has drunk the Prince's blood three times. Now, every time the Prince says "Do this," you feel an almost irresistible urge to obey. Even if the order is clearly dangerous or runs against your Convictions, you must make a roll to disobey. And yet, you still understand it's wrong — you simply **cannot stop yourself**.

**Advice for Storyteller and Player**  
The Blood Bond is one of the most powerful tools of drama in V5. Use it not just as a mechanic, but as a source of personal tragedy. Many chronicles are built entirely around a character trying to break free of a Bond, or, conversely, using one to tie someone important to themselves.

---

**Use of Psychoactive Substances**

Some vampires continue to use drugs, alcohol, or other substances through their victims' blood. This can grant temporary bonuses, but often leads to serious problems.

**Mechanics**  
Specify a **particular substance** (alcohol, cocaine, heroin, LSD, marijuana, etc.).

- **Flaw (••) Addiction**  
  If the blood you most recently drank did not contain your chosen substance, the pool for **all** rolls (except those for immediately satisfying the addiction) is reduced by **2d10**.

- **Flaw (•) Habit**  
  The same, but the penalty is **-1d10**.

- **• High-Functioning Addict**  
  If the necessary substance is present in the blood, you gain **+1d10** on rolls in one chosen Attribute category (Physical / Social / Mental) for the entire scene.

**Important Rules**
- The substance's effect lasts until you fully sate the Hunger, or drink blood without that substance.
- Ventrue with the "Discerning Taste" Bane may have serious problems with this merit/flaw.
- Addiction is an excellent source of drama and story hooks (the search for the "right" blood, conflicts with dealers, and so on).

**Example:**  
Your character is a **High-Functioning Addict** (cocaine). When they drink the blood of someone on cocaine, they gain +1d10 on **Social** rolls. But if they feed on "clean" blood for several nights, they become irritable and suffer a -2d10 penalty on all rolls.

---

**Folkloric Merits and Flaws**

Some vampires suffer from (or possess) traits that mortals consider classic "vampire" weaknesses. In V5 these are rare, but can heavily affect the game.

- **Flaw (••) Stake Bait**  
  A stake through the heart causes **Final Death** instead of torpor.

- **Flaw (•) Folkloric Bane**  
  You take **Aggravated damage** from one folkloric source: ultraviolet light (the sun), silver, or holy water.

- **Flaw (•) Folkloric Block** (chosen separately)  
  You are physically unable to pass a specific obstacle: a sacred symbol, running water, the threshold of a home without an invitation, garlic, scattered seeds, and so on.

- **Flaw (•) Stigmata**  
  At Hunger 4+, bleeding wounds (stigmata) appear on your body, which are hard to hide.

- **•• Eat Food**  
  You can eat and drink ordinary food, enjoy its taste, and even imitate a normal life. However, before resting for the day, you **must** purge everything you've eaten, or things will get extremely unpleasant.

**Roleplaying Advice**  
Folkloric flaws are an excellent way to add uniqueness and vulnerability to a character. They are especially atmospheric in chronicles with a strong Gothic or classic-vampire flavor. That said, don't take too many — they can make survival considerably harder.

###### THIN-BLOOD MERITS AND FLAWS
###### THIN-BLOOD MERITS AND FLAWS

Thin-bloods (14th–16th generation) are a special category of vampire. They have significant advantages over full-blooded Kindred (the ability to walk in the sun, eat normal food, suffer less from fire, and so on). Because of this, their balance system works differently.

**Core rule:**  
**Every Thin-blood Merit must be balanced by a matching Flaw.**  
You cannot take only the upsides — you must always take the corresponding downside.

This reflects the idea that their blood grants freedom while bringing new problems at the same time.

**Thin-blood Merits**

- **Alchemist**  
  You are a master of Thin-Blood Alchemy. You can craft powerful potions and concoctions that mimic the effects of other clans' Disciplines.

- **Day Hunter**  
  You tolerate sunlight far better than other vampires. You can be active by day with minimal consequences (mild burns instead of Aggravated damage).

- **Vampiric Resilience**  
  You gain some of the benefits of a full-blooded vampire (you heal wounds faster, resist disease better), even though you remain thin-blooded.

- **Camarilla Patron**  
  You have an influential patron among the traditionalists, who protects you from persecution for being "incomplete."

- **Full of Life**  
  You look and feel far more "alive" than ordinary vampires. It's easier to maintain the Masquerade and social ties.

- **Bloodline Potential**  
  You have a chance to eventually lower your generation over time (becoming a full 13th-generation vampire).

- **Discipline Affinity**  
  You learn one chosen Discipline faster and more cheaply.

- **Friend to the Anarchs**  
  You have good relations with the Anarchs — they see you as "one of their own" and help you more readily.

**Thin-blood Flaws**

- **Toothless**
  Your fangs are barely developed. Your bite is far less effective.
  
- **Vitae Dependency**
  You need to regularly drink other vampires' blood, or you grow weaker.
  
- **Clan Curse**
  You've still inherited the Bane of one of the clans (even if formally a Caitiff).
  
- **Camarilla Branding**
  Traditionalists treat you with contempt and distrust.
  
- **Dead Flesh**
  Your body looks and smells more like a corpse than usual.

- **Unbridled Beast**
  Your Beast is far more aggressive and harder to control.

- **Anarch Outcast**
  The Anarchs consider you a "traitor" or a weakling.

- **Mortal Frailty**
  You take Aggravated damage from ordinary weapons far too easily.

**Roleplay Example**  
Your character is a thin-blood who took "Day Hunter" and "Full of Life." By day they can walk around the city in relative peace, even hold down a remote job. But because of "Dead Flesh," their skin looks sickly pale, and they carry a faint scent of the grave. So they always wear thick clothes with a hood, and use perfume. This creates constant tension: "I can live almost like a human... but people can still sense something's wrong with me."

**Advice for Storyteller and Player**  
Thin-bloods are an excellent choice for anyone who wants to play "a human among monsters," or explore the question of "what it means to be a vampire when you're barely one at all." Their merits and flaws create very strong roleplaying potential and interesting conflicts with Kindred society.

**Haven**

Your **Haven** is your personal safe place for the daysleep. It protects you from the sun, enemies, and accidental witnesses. A good haven is one of the most critical factors in a vampire's survival.

**Mechanics:**  
A basic Haven is bought with Merit dots. The higher the rating, the larger and better-protected your lair.

**Basic Haven Levels**

| Dots | Level                        | Description |
|-------|--------------------------------|----------|
| **•** | Small and secure            | An apartment, basement, or small house. Minimal comfort and protection. |
| **••** | Large and comfortable          | Spacious housing (a loft, a mansion, a large basement). Room for storing belongings and hosting guests. |
| **•••** | Vast and well-protected | An entire building, a bunker, or a mansion with good security. Considerable safety. |

**Additional Haven Qualities** (bought separately, usually 1–2 dots each)

**Merits:**

- **Library** — a collection of books, documents, or digital media. Useful for research into occultism, history, or politics.
- **Laboratory** — an equipped space for scientific, alchemical, or medical experiments.
- **Cell** — a secure room for holding prisoners or guests (soundproofed, with reinforced doors).
- **Operating Room** — a fully equipped medical room for performing complex surgery (including on vampires).
- **Security** — permanent physical protection (armed people, ghouls, or mercenaries).
- **Advanced Security System** — modern cameras, sensors, electronic locks, and alarm systems.
- **Mystical Protection** — rituals and wards that make infiltration via Disciplines or magic harder.
- **Escape Routes** — secret exits, underground passages, or multiple evacuation options.
- **Luxury** — high-class furnishings, comfort, and status. Has a good effect on social interactions within the haven.
- **Weapon Cache** — a hidden stash of firearms, melee weapons, ammunition, and explosives.
- **Exclusive Location** — a particularly fortunate spot (sewers beneath the city, a downtown penthouse, an abandoned factory, an island, and so on).

**Haven Flaws:**

- **Creepy Place** — the haven creates an oppressive, frightening impression. Guests, and even other Kindred, feel uncomfortable there (social penalties).
- **Supernatural Nuisance** — strange phenomena occur in the haven (ghosts, poltergeists, the weakening of certain Disciplines, the attraction of unwanted entities).

**A Note for the Storyteller**  
A player may propose their own haven quality that isn't on this list. If it's logical and balanced, allow it. The main rule: the cooler and more unusual the haven, the more potential weaknesses and story hooks it should carry.

**Example:**  
Your character has a Haven ••• with the "Mystical Protection," "Library," and "Escape Routes" qualities. It's an excellent lair for a Tremere occultist. But because of "Creepy Place," ordinary guests feel uncomfortable there, and the Storyteller may occasionally throw in mystical events inside the haven.

**Roleplay Advice:**  
A haven isn't just a "base." It's your second home, your fortress, and at the same time your greatest weakness. Many chronicles begin or end with an assault on a haven.

#### 8. Disciplines
**Disciplines** are the supernatural powers granted to you by Caine's cursed blood. They are what turns an ordinary corpse into a true Kindred, and determine **what** sets you apart among other vampires.

Choose **only from your clan's in-clan Disciplines** (see the **03. Clans and Bloodlines** section). Most clans have three. Caitiff and Thin-bloods have their own special rules (see below).

**Distributing Dots at Character Creation:**

- You get **3 dots total** in Disciplines.
- Distribute them as follows: **one Discipline at 2 dots**, **the second at 1 dot**.
- You **cannot** take a third in-clan Discipline at the start (it can be learned later with experience).
- A maximum of **2 dots** in any one Discipline at the start.

**Choosing Powers:**
- For every dot in a Discipline, you choose **one power** of that level or lower.
- Many Disciplines offer several power options at the same level — choose carefully, as they heavily shape your style of play.
- Example: with **Celerity ••**, you can take two different level-1 powers, or one level-2 power.

**Example In-Clan Disciplines (briefly):**

- **Brujah**: Celerity, Potence, often also Presence
- **Ventrue**: Dominate, Fortitude, Presence
- **Gangrel**: Animalism, Protean, Fortitude
- **Malkavian**: Auspex, Dominate, Obfuscate
- **Nosferatu**: Obfuscate, Animalism, Potence
- **Toreador**: Presence, Celerity, Auspex
- **Tremere**: Blood Sorcery, Auspex, Dominate

##### Special Cases

**Caitiff**  
You have no fixed in-clan Disciplines. You may choose **any two Disciplines** (even ones that are out-of-clan for everyone else). This grants enormous flexibility, but often draws suspicion within Kindred society.

**Thin-bloods (14th–15th generation)**  
You have almost no permanent access to ordinary Disciplines. You may use **Thin-Blood Alchemy** and temporarily gain the powers of other Disciplines through blood Resonance. If you take the "Discipline Affinity" merit, you can learn one chosen Discipline faster.

**Predator Type**  
Some Predator Types grant an **extra dot** in a Discipline (+1). This can raise one Discipline to 3 at character creation — very powerful.

**Important Rules and Tips:**
- Disciplines are **not just super-powers**. They define how you solve problems: through raw force (Potence), cunning (Obfuscate), control (Dominate), or terror (Oblivion).
- Choose them to fit your concept and clan. A Tremere scholar without Blood Sorcery looks strange.
- At the start, it's better to take 2–3 levels in 1–2 Disciplines than to spread a single dot across many.
- Later, with experience, you can raise your Disciplines (in-clan ones are cheaper) and learn new ones.
- Remember the **Hunger**: many powers require spending blood (a Rouse Check), which raises Hunger and the risk of Frenzy.

**Example Distributions:**

1. **A Brutal Gangrel Alleycat**  
   - Protean •• (Claws + one level-1 power)  
   - Potence • (one power)

2. **A Charismatic Toreador Siren**  
   - Presence ••  
   - Celerity • 

3. **A Tremere Occultist**  
   - Blood Sorcery ••  
   - Auspex •

**Recommendation:**  
After choosing your Disciplines, go back to your concept and ask: "How do these powers reflect my story and my tragedy?" The most memorable characters are the ones whose powers deepen their inner conflict (for example, a Toreador with high Presence who fears losing their beauty and their humanity).

#### 9. Convictions

**Convictions** are the moral rules your character tries to live by, even after the Embrace. They are one of the most important tools V5 offers for roleplaying **Humanity**.

- **Convictions** are your personal, deeply held beliefs.  
- **Values** is the broader term, covering Convictions plus Touchstones.

You choose **exactly 3 Convictions** at character creation.

##### What Makes a Good Conviction?

A good Conviction is:
- Personal and specific (not a vague phrase like "be a good person").
- A source of inner conflict with your vampiric nature.
- Tied to a **Touchstone**.

**Examples of Strong Convictions:**
- "Never betray those who truly trust me"
- "Family (by blood or by choice) comes above all else"
- "The weak must be protected, even when it's dangerous"
- "A promise made to a child must never be broken"
- "Never kill for food" (a very heavy Conviction)
- "Always pay my debts"

##### Mechanical Effect of Convictions

Convictions are directly tied to the **Humanity** system:

- **Upholding a Conviction** → helps preserve Humanity.
- **Breaking a Conviction** → the character gains **Stains** — "blemishes" on their Humanity.

If you break your Conviction, the Storyteller may give you 1 to 3 Stains, depending on the severity of the breach. As Stains accumulate, Humanity falls, and the Beast increasingly gains the upper hand.

**Important:**  
Convictions aren't just "roleplay." They are **mechanics**. They literally measure how much of your humanity remains.

##### Recommendations for Choosing

- Choose Convictions that are **hard** to uphold in a vampire's world.
- One Conviction can be "easy," one "moderate," and one "very hard" — this creates excellent drama.
- Convictions can change over the course of the chronicle (usually after significant events), but that, too, requires roleplaying it out.

---

#### 10. Touchstones

**Touchstones** are the most important mortals (or sometimes places/ideas) in your character's life, the ones that remind them who they were, and what's worth fighting the Beast for.

Every Conviction **must** be tied to at least one **Touchstone**. So you'll have a minimum of **3 Touchstones** at character creation.

##### What Makes a Good Touchstone?

A good Touchstone is:
- A specific person (not "all children" or "humanity" in general).
- Emotionally connected to the character.
- Easily put in danger, or used against you.
- A reflection of the Conviction it's tied to.

**Examples of Conviction + Touchstone Pairings:**

- **Conviction:** "Family comes above all else"  
  **Touchstone:** A younger sister who believes you're missing.

- **Conviction:** "Never betray those who trust me"  
  **Touchstone:** An old mortal friend who still thinks you simply changed.

- **Conviction:** "The weak must be protected"  
  **Touchstone:** A teenager from a rough neighborhood you once pulled out of trouble.

##### Touchstone Mechanics

- If a Touchstone **suffers**, or is put in danger because of your actions → you gain **Stains**.
- If a Touchstone **dies**, or you **betray** them → loss of the Conviction, plus a large number of Stains.
- If the Touchstone is safe, and you act in accordance with the Conviction → this helps you hold on to your Humanity.

**A Touchstone can be:**
- A mortal person (the most common option).
- A place (an old house, a mother's grave, a favorite park).
- An idea / group (weaker, and less often recommended).

##### Recommendations

- Don't make all your Touchstones "perfect." Let some of them be difficult, conflicted relationships.
- Touchstones are one of the main sources of drama in V5. The Storyteller will use them.
- Over the course of the chronicle, a Touchstone can be replaced (through roleplay and a Humanity check) if the old one is lost or stops mattering.

**Example:**  
**Conviction:** "Never kill for food"  
**Touchstone:** An old literature teacher who still believes in you, and thinks you're "a good kid."

#### 11. Final Touches

Now that the foundation of your character is ready, it's time to add the last important details that will make them feel alive and memorable.

- **Name and age at death**  
- **Appearance** (how you look, how you dress, your habits)  
- **Biography before the Embrace** (brief, but important — what shaped you as a person)  
- **Chronicle** (your personal history since the Embrace — what's happened to you since)  
- **Ambition and Desire**  
- **Filling out the character sheet**

---

##### Ambition and Desire

These two elements are a crucial part of both mechanics and roleplay in V5. They define **what your character wants**, and what can distract them.

**Ambition**  
A big, long-term goal that your character is willing to risk their unlife and their Humanity for.

**Examples of Ambition:**
- Become the Prince of this city
- Find and destroy your Sire
- Discover a way to be free of the Hunger
- Make your coterie the most influential power in the domain
- Regain your mortality

**Reward:** When a character makes significant progress toward, or achieves, their Ambition, the Storyteller usually awards **1–3 experience points** (most often 2).

---

**Desire**  
An immediate, powerful urge that can easily distract you from important matters. Usually changes every few sessions.

**Examples of Desire:**
- Win the approval of a specific influential Kindred
- Always be the most beautiful/desired person in the room
- Never miss a new exhibition/concert/premiere
- Find a rare edition of a book / an artifact
- Drink the blood of someone with a specific Resonance

**Reward:** If, over the course of a session, you **actively** pursued your Desire and it created interesting moments or conflicts, the Storyteller may award **1 experience point** for that session.

**Important Nuances:**
- The reward is **not automatic** — it depends on the quality of the roleplay, and how much the Ambition/Desire generated drama.
- An Ambition can be changed once every few sessions (usually after significant progress or a major setback).
- A well-chosen pairing of "Ambition + Desire" creates a powerful inner conflict.

**Example of a Complete Concept with Final Touches:**
"A young investigative journalist, Embraced after getting too close to the Prince's secret.  
**Archetype:** Truth-Seeker | **Nature:** Rebel | **Demeanor:** A professionally ironic journalist.  
**Ambition:** Expose and destroy corruption in the city.  
**Desire:** Win recognition and attention from influential people."

---

#### 12. Building a Coterie

A coterie is **your family by night**. In V5, the coterie matters far more than just being "a group of players." It's the core social unit through which you'll experience the chronicle. Most personal drama and conflict unfolds through relationships within the coterie.

A coterie is not a random assortment of vampires. It's a group that survives together, sharing risk, secrets, and blood.

##### Why the Coterie Matters So Much

- You hunt and protect each other together.
- You share a domain (or are forced to poach on someone else's).
- You share fame, disgrace, and the consequences of your actions.
- Relationships within the coterie are often more important than your relationship with the Prince or your clan.

##### Types of Coteries (Examples)

- **Coterie of Choice** — you decided to stick together yourselves (friends, lovers, like-minded souls).
- **Coterie of Necessity** — a shared threat or an order from above brought you together.
- **Family Coterie** — bound by blood or a shared past.
- **Combat Coterie** — a group of mercenaries or warriors.
- **Political Coterie** — conspirators, or a faction reaching for power.
- **Nomadic Coterie** — constantly on the move, not tied to a single domain.

##### Step-by-Step Coterie Creation

1. **Discuss the coterie's concept**  
   What's your shared style and goal? (survival, revenge, power, the search for Golconda, protecting a neighborhood, and so on)

2. **Choose a coterie type**  
   This will help you understand how you interact with the city and other Kindred.

3. **Determine the coterie's status in the city**  
   - Do you have an **official domain** (granted by the Prince or Baron)?  
   - Are you **poachers** (hunting on someone else's territory)?  
   - Are you an **independent group** (Autarkis)?

4. **Assign roles within the coterie**  
   (not necessarily rigid, but useful for understanding each member's strengths)

5. **Create shared coterie merits**  
   (a shared Haven, a shared Herd, shared Allies, and so on)

6. **Build connections between the characters**  
   (the final, and very important, step)

##### Connections Between Player Characters

After creating your individual characters, be sure to discuss:

- How did you meet?
- Why do you specifically stick together?
- What unites you, and what could tear you apart?
- Are there debts, secrets, tension, romantic feelings, or rivalry between any of you?

**Recommended Questions for Building Connections:**
- Who saved whose life?
- Who owes whom a major favor?
- Who had a conflict with whom in the past?
- Is there a secret crush or jealousy among you?
- Who does everyone consider the coterie's leader?

**Tip:**  
Strong connections within a coterie are fuel for drama. The more tension and personal stakes between you, the more interesting the game will be.

---

#### Polishing Your Character

Now that your character's foundation is ready, add the last important details:

- Name and age at death
- Appearance and mannerisms
- Biography before the Embrace (brief, but meaningful)
- Chronicle (what has happened to you since the Embrace)
- **Ambition** and **Desire**
- Fill out the character sheet

---

##### Character Creation Checklist

Before you finish, be sure to check that everything is ready:

**Be sure to check:**
- [ ] You have a **clear Concept** (who you are, and why you're interesting to watch)
- [ ] You've chosen a **Clan** (or Caitiff / Thin-blood) and a **Sire**
- [ ] **Attributes** are distributed (4 / 3×3 / 2×4 / 1)
- [ ] **13 Skill dots** + specialties are distributed
- [ ] You've chosen **3 Convictions**
- [ ] **3 Discipline dots** are distributed
- [ ] Every Conviction has a **Touchstone**
- [ ] **Merits** are chosen (7 dots + a maximum of +2 from Flaws)
- [ ] A **Predator Type** is chosen
- [ ] **Ambition** and **Desire** are filled in
- [ ] You have a **connection to the coterie** (how you met, why you're together, what your relationships are like)

**Recommended to check:**
- [ ] The character fits the **Triad** (Character — Coterie — Chronicle)
- [ ] The character has an **inner conflict** (Humanity vs. the Beast)
- [ ] There is at least one **weakness** (a 1-dot Attribute, a serious Flaw, a heavy Conviction)
- [ ] The character is interesting **to you**, the player

**Final Tip:**  
If, after filling out the entire sheet, you can answer in one or two sentences the question "Why will it be interesting to watch this character over the next several years?" — then the concept has succeeded.

## 05. Quick NPCs and Antagonists

Not every character in the game needs a full sheet like a player's. For most passersby, minor Kindred, werewolves, ghosts, and other creatures, it's more convenient to use **simplified templates**.

This section helps the Storyteller quickly build antagonists, allies, and neutral NPCs without spending hours fleshing out every single one.

### When to Use Quick NPCs

- Minor characters and chance encounters
- Combat opponents (thugs, Inquisition hunters, ghouls)
- Short-term antagonists
- Background Kindred and mortals
- Monsters and supernatural creatures

### Basic NPC Templates

#### 1. Ordinary Mortal (an average person)
- **Health:** 4–6
- **Willpower:** 3–5
- **Key pools:** 4–6 dice in their own field, 2–3 in everything else
- **Example:** a street thug, a police officer, an office clerk, a doctor

#### 2. Dangerous Mortal (a professional / hunter)
- **Health:** 6–8
- **Willpower:** 5–7
- **Key pools:** 7–9 dice (firearms, investigation, brawling)
- **Example:** a Second Inquisition agent, a vampire hunter, a special-forces operative

#### 3. Rank-and-File Kindred (neonate / ancilla)
- **Health:** 7
- **Willpower:** 5–6
- **Blood Potency:** 1
- **Key Disciplines:** 1–2 levels (1–2 powers)
- **Example:** an ordinary Camarilla or Anarch neonate

#### 4. Dangerous Kindred (elder / threat)
- **Health:** 8–10
- **Willpower:** 7–9
- **Blood Potency:** 3+
- **Key Disciplines:** 3–4 levels
- **Example:** a Primogen, a Baron, an ancient hunter

#### 5. Supernatural Creatures (briefly)

Here are simplified templates for the most common non-player supernatural creatures. They're meant for quick use by the Storyteller.

##### Werewolf (Lupine)

**An extremely dangerous opponent.** In hybrid form, a true monster.

- **Health:** 10–12  
- **Willpower:** 8–10  
- **Key pools:**  
  - Combat (hybrid form): 10–12 dice  
  - Survival / Tracking: 9–11 dice  
- **Traits:**  
  - Regeneration (heals 1 Aggravated damage per turn)  
  - Powerful claw and bite damage (+3–4)  
  - Suffers no wound penalties until reaching a critical state  
- **Weaknesses:** Silver (deals Aggravated damage), certain rituals  

**Tip:** A single werewolf is already a serious threat to a small coterie.

---

##### Ghost (Wraith)

**An immaterial being.** Dangerous psychologically and through possession.

- **Health:** Not applicable (immaterial)  
- **Willpower:** 6–10  
- **Key pools:**  
  - Possession / Poltergeist: 7–9 dice  
  - Intimidation / Deception: 8–10 dice  
- **Traits:**  
  - Can possess people  
  - Deals stress (damage to Willpower)  
  - Sees the hidden, and reads emotions  
- **Weaknesses:** Exorcism, salt, cold iron, certain rituals  

**Tip:** Ghosts work excellently as a source of paranoia and long-term story arcs.

---

##### Ghoul

**A mortal who drinks vampire blood.** A loyal servant or bodyguard.

- **Health:** 6–8  
- **Willpower:** 5–7  
- **Key pools:**  
  - Combat: 6–9 dice (depending on the master)  
  - Loyalty to master: very high  
- **Traits:**  
  - Doesn't age, as long as they keep receiving vitae  
  - +1 level in one of the master's Disciplines  
  - Heals wounds faster  
- **Weaknesses:** Severe dependence on the master's blood (withdrawal if deprived)  

**Tip:** Ghouls are excellent "meat shields" and spies. Their death is often a powerful emotional blow to their master.

##### Mage (Mortal Sorcerer / Hedge Mage)

- **Health:** 4–6  
- **Willpower:** 8–10  
- **Key pools:**  
  - Occult / Rituals: 8–12 dice  
  - Social / Deception: 6–8 dice  
- **Traits:**  
  - Powerful rituals and spells  
  - Can create wards and curses  
  - Often possesses artifacts  
- **Weaknesses:** Physically very weak, vulnerable to direct violence, risk of "Paradox" from crude magic  

**Tip:** Mages rarely fight in the open. They weave intrigue, set traps, and use others as their weapons.

## 06. The Nature of Vampires
### What It Means to Be Kindred

You are no longer human. But you are not dead, either, not in the full sense of the word. You are **Kindred** — a living contradiction, a creature frozen between life and death.

Your body is dead, but it moves. Your heart doesn't beat, your lungs don't truly breathe, the blood in your veins is cold and thick. You no longer feel cold or heat the way you used to, you don't need food or air — but in exchange, the **Hunger** has become the one truly real sensation that never leaves you for long.

#### A Kindred's Physiology (the "Walking Corpse")

- You do not age, and you do not catch ordinary diseases.
- Your body produces no heat — on thermal imaging, you appear as a cold patch (a "Blankbody").
- You can eat and drink, but it brings no pleasure and requires later purging (except with special merits).
- Wounds heal only through blood. Without it, you gradually wither and slip into torpor.
- You still feel pain, emotion, pleasure, and fear — it's just that all of it is now muted and distorted by the Beast.

#### Myths and Reality

| Myth                              | Reality |
|----------------------------------|----------|
| Crosses, garlic, and holy water cause harm | Only if you genuinely believe it yourself (a Folkloric Block) |
| Vampires don't reflect in mirrors   | They do reflect (though some very old Kindred don't, and clans like the Lasombra don't) |
| Vampires can't go out by day        | Thin-bloods can. Elders burn. |
| A bite turns you into a vampire        | You need to drain a person almost completely, then give them your blood |
| Vampires can't enter a home without an invitation | This only works if you yourself believe in the rule (a Folkloric Block) |

You are a predator forced to wear human skin. And the longer you exist, the thinner that skin grows.

---

### The Hunger and the Hunger Pool

The **Hunger** is the heart of vampiric nature. It is not simply thirst. It is a constant, gnawing, animal craving that never fully disappears. Even when you're sated, you feel it somewhere at the edge of your mind.

#### What the Hunger Is

Hunger is measured from **0 to 5**:

- **0** — a rare state of full satiation.
- **1–2** — a comfortable level for most Kindred.
- **3** — already noticeable. You grow irritable, and concentration becomes harder.
- **4** — heavy discomfort. The Beast starts whispering in your ear.
- **5** — the critical mark. The Beast is tearing to get out.

#### How the Hunger Pool Works

Whenever you make a roll, some of your ordinary dice are replaced with **red Hunger dice**.

- The higher your Hunger, the more red dice are in the pool.
- Red dice work like ordinary dice, **but** they carry a risk:
  - Rolling a **1** on a red die often leads to complications, or to failures with heavy consequences.
  - Rolling a **10** can produce an especially vivid, or savage, success.

#### Frenzy: the Beast's Grip

When Hunger reaches **5**, or under severe stress/provocation, a character risks falling into **Frenzy**.

- **Rage Frenzy** — blind aggression and violence.
- **Fear Frenzy** — panicked terror and the urge to flee/hide.
- **Hunger Frenzy** (less common) — cold, calculating, monstrously effective predation.

During Frenzy, control of the character passes, partly or fully, to the Storyteller.

#### How to Sate the Hunger

- **Partial feeding** — lowers Hunger by 1 point.
- **Full satiation** — requires drinking a significant amount of blood (usually almost to the last drop).
- **The Rouse Check** — when you try to stop early, you roll a die. On a failure, Hunger doesn't drop, or even rises.

**Important:** Even if you fully sate your Hunger down to 0, after a while it will slowly start climbing again. There is no way to be rid of the Hunger entirely — it is the curse of your nature.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
Hunger should always be felt. Even at level 2, a good player shows slight restlessness, licking their fangs, distraction. At level 4, the character is already fighting themself. At 5, they're on the edge of losing control.

---

### Blood and Its Power

Blood is the single most important thing you possess. It replaces your heart, your breath, and your soul. It grants power, fuels your Disciplines, and is, at the same time, your greatest curse.

#### Generation

**Generation** is a measure of how far your blood lies from Caine. The lower the generation number, the stronger and more ancient the blood.

- **13th generation** — the standard for most modern vampires.
- **14th–16th generations** — **Thin-bloods**. The blood is very weak, but grants unique abilities (walking by day, eating normal food, and so on).
- **12th and below** — ancient, mighty blood. Such vampires are rare, and usually very influential.

The lower the generation, the higher the **Blood Potency**, the stronger your Disciplines, and the more blood you need to survive.

#### Blood Potency

**Blood Potency** is the true power of your vitae. It determines what you're capable of as a vampire.

**What Blood Potency Grants:**

- More blood can be spent per turn.
- Stronger Discipline effects.
- Higher maximum trait values.
- Better resistance to certain effects (such as a Blood Bond from a younger vampire).
- More effective healing.

| Blood Potency | Generation (approx.) | Key Capabilities |
|------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 0          | 14–16 (Thin-bloods) | Nearly human limitations |
| 1          | 13                   | A standard vampire |
| 2–3        | 11–12                | Noticeably stronger than average |
| 4–5        | 9–10                 | Elder-level |
| 6+         | 8 and below             | Ancient monsters |

#### Resonance and Dyscrasias

Blood carries the **emotional imprint** of the person you're feeding from. This imprint is called **Resonance**.

**The Four Main Types of Resonance:**

- **Sanguine** (anger, passion, excitement) → enhances Presence, Potence, Celerity.
- **Choleric** (anger, rage, envy) → enhances Potence and Celerity.
- **Melancholic** (sadness, nostalgia, apathy) → enhances Obfuscate, Fortitude, Auspex.
- **Phlegmatic** (calm, cold calculation) → enhances Dominate, Auspex.

A **Dyscrasia** is a **strong, concentrated** Resonance. If you spend a long time deliberately "preparing" a vessel (through torment, manipulation, or a long relationship), you can gain a powerful temporary bonus, or even a unique effect.

**Example:**  
You've drunk the blood of someone in a state of intense anger (Choleric Resonance) for several nights in a row. With enough concentration, you might gain a temporary bonus to damage or pain resistance.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
Resonance isn't just "a bonus to dice." It's a chance to show how a vampire **affects** their victims, and how their emotions begin to poison the vampire in turn.

---

### Blood Bonds and Diablerie

Blood doesn't just nourish — it **binds**. The two most powerful and dangerous blood-related mechanics are the **Blood Bond** and **Diablerie**.

#### The Blood Bond

The Blood Bond is a mystical, almost irresistible feeling of attachment and submission that arises when one vampire drinks another's blood.

**How a Bond Forms**

For a Bond to form, you must drink the **same** Kindred's blood **three times** (on different nights). Every additional drink strengthens the connection.

**Bond Levels** (1 through 6)

| Level | Strength of Attachment                     | Effect |
|---------|----------------------------------------|--------|
| 1       | Mild fondness                        | A wish to please |
| 2       | Strong attachment                  | Hard to refuse |
| 3       | Deep devotion                   | Nearly impossible to disobey |
| 4–6     | Absolute slavery                     | Willing to die or kill on command |

**Resistance Mechanics**

To disobey a direct order from their regnant, the thrall undergoes a **contest**:
- **Resolve + Intelligence** (thrall) against **Bond Strength** (regnant).

The higher the Bond's level, the harder it is to resist.

**Important Rules**
- Bonds only work **in one direction** (the thrall is bound to the regnant, never the other way around).
- One vampire can hold several Bonds at the same time.
- A Bond can weaken if you go a long time without the regnant's blood, but this is very difficult.

#### Diablerie (the Amaranth)

Diablerie is the act of **fully devouring another Kindred**, soul included. The gravest crime among the Kindred.

**How It's Done**
1. The victim must be fully restrained or drained of strength.
2. They must be drained completely dry.
3. Then comes the drinking of the **final drop of vitae** — the soul itself.

**Consequences of Diablerie**
- A drop in generation (if the victim was older than you).
- An automatic loss of **1 Humanity**.
- Black veins in the aura (visible through Auspex) — lasting anywhere from a year to decades.
- Risk of being overwhelmed by the victim's soul (especially if they were significantly more powerful).

**How Different Communities View Diablerie**

- **The Camarilla** — considers Diablerie one of the gravest crimes there is. It almost always results in a **Blood Hunt**. Officially forbidden even to elders.
- **The Anarchs** — more tolerant, especially if the victim was an elder or a tyrant. Many Anarchs see Diablerie as a tool of the "War of Ages." Still, they condemn it if it causes problems for the coterie.
- **The Sabbat** — Diablerie is encouraged, and even ritualized. Considered a sacred act of absorbing power and drawing closer to the Dark Father.
- **The Ministry (Setites)** — use Diablerie as a tool of enlightenment or destruction. Their attitude is pragmatic: "if it serves the Serpent, it's right."
- **The Tremere** — extremely negative. Diablerie committed against a Tremere is considered one of the clan's most terrible crimes. They actively hunt diablerists.
- **The Hecata** — wary. Devouring a soul can disturb the balance with the world of the dead, and draw unwanted attention from ghosts.
- **Thin-bloods and Caitiff** — for them, Diablerie is often the **only way** to lower their generation and become a "true" vampire. Many thin-bloods dream of it, but fear the consequences.

**Roleplaying Tip**  
Diablerie isn't just "gaining power." It's a heavy moral crime that leaves scars on the soul. Many chronicles are built around the question: "Is power worth a price like this?"

---

### Humanity and the Downward Spiral

**Humanity** is a measure of how much of a person you remain, despite the curse. It isn't simply "kindness," but a connection to your former life: the capacity to feel guilt, love, shame, compassion, and a sense of meaning in existence.

The lower your Humanity, the more the **Beast** controls you.

#### The Humanity Scale

- **8–10** — You're very close to human. Capable of genuine love, deep remorse, and moral anguish.
- **5–7** — An average level. You still feel human, but already sense the Beast's pressure.
- **3–4** — You're beginning to turn into a monster. Emotions grow numb, your body looks more dead.
- **1–2** — You're on the edge. Most people instinctively sense danger in you.
- **0** — **Final Fall**. The Beast takes full control. The character becomes an NPC, run by the Storyteller.

#### How Humanity Is Lost

Humanity falls when you:
- Break your **Convictions** (you gain **Stains**).
- Commit grave moral crimes (murder, betraying loved ones, Diablerie, and so on).
- Ignore your Touchstones for a long time.

Every significant breach can bring **1 to 3 Stains**. Once enough Stains accumulate, Humanity drops by 1 point.

#### How Humanity Is Raised

Raising Humanity is **far harder** than losing it. It always requires significant effort and roleplay.

**The Main Ways to Raise It:**
- **Long-term adherence to Convictions**, and caring for your Touchstones (especially saving a Touchstone who is in danger).
- **Significant acts of humanity** (saving the innocent, giving up power for the sake of morality, atoning for a grave crime).
- **The search for Golconda**, or deep spiritual/philosophical work on oneself.
- **Special events in the chronicle** (the Storyteller may reward an outstanding roleplaying moment with a rise in Humanity).

**Rule:** A rise in Humanity should always be **difficult** and **meaningful**. It usually happens no more often than once every several months of real game time.

#### The Downward Spiral

The lower your Humanity, the harder it becomes to keep from falling further:
- You break your Convictions more easily.
- The Beast grows stronger, and takes control more often.
- Your body and behavior grow ever more monstrous.
- Social ties to other people fray on their own.

This is what's known as the **Downward Spiral**.

#### Golconda

**Golconda** is a legendary state of enlightenment in which a vampire achieves harmony with their Beast, and ceases to be its slave.

- Very few Kindred are said to have ever reached this state.
- Those who achieve Golconda need almost no blood, control their Beast far better, and find inner peace.
- The search for Golconda is one of the deepest, most tragic personal stories in the game.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
A fall in Humanity should feel gradual and painful. A good player shows how their character slowly loses the ability to love, to cry, or to enjoy simple things. A rise, meanwhile, should be a rare, hard-won, and deeply significant event.

---

### Torpor and Final Death

Even immortal Kindred can be brought down. There are two main states that can "remove" a vampire from play: **Torpor** and **Final Death**.

#### Torpor

Torpor is a deep, death-like sleep that a vampire falls into from severe wounds, severe blood loss, or by their own will.

**When Torpor Sets In:**
- The Health track is fully filled with Aggravated damage.
- Severe blood loss combined with heavy wounds.
- Certain rituals, Discipline effects, or a voluntary descent (to wait out danger).

**Duration:**
- From a few nights (young vampires) to decades, or even centuries (the ancient).
- The higher the **Blood Potency**, the longer and deeper torpor can be.

**How to Come Out of Torpor:**
- **On your own** — in the presence of grave danger, or the scent of blood (requires a roll).
- **From outside** — another Kindred can wake you by feeding you their vitae (usually 1–3 Blood points).
- **Time** — torpor eventually ends on its own once its duration has passed.

While in torpor, the body looks like a true corpse, and is extremely vulnerable.


**Waking from Torpor on Your Own**

A vampire can try to wake from torpor on their own, but it isn't easy.

**Mechanics:**

The base roll is: Resolve + Composure (sometimes Resolve + Stamina, at the Storyteller's discretion).
Difficulty is usually 3–5, depending on the circumstances:
- 3 — if there's a very strong stimulus (for example, someone is trying to burn or drain you).
- 4 — a standard danger (a fire, enemies nearby, loud noise).
- 5+ — deep torpor combined with a weak stimulus.


**What Grants a Bonus to Waking:**

The scent of fresh blood (especially at high Hunger).
Severe physical danger (you're being burned, hacked at, and so on).
Emotionally significant events (a sire's scream, a Touchstone in danger, and so on).

**Important Nuances:**

Waking on your own from deep torpor (ancient vampires, low Humanity) is extremely difficult.
Every failed roll can lengthen the torpor or inflict additional Stains.
Young vampires (13th–14th generation) wake more easily than elders.

#### Final Death

This is the true, irreversible end of a vampire.

**Ways to Cause Final Death:**
- Beheading.
- Fully burning the body.
- Taking an enormous amount of Aggravated damage (especially from the sun, or a powerful fire).
- Diablerie (being drained completely, to the last drop).
- Certain rare rituals or artifacts.

After Final Death, the character **dies for good**. They do not automatically become a ghost.

**Can a Player Continue as a Ghost (Wraith)?**

**Theoretically — yes, but extremely rarely.**  
This is only possible with the full agreement of the entire group and the Storyteller. Playing as a ghost requires a separate set of mechanics, a different value system, and changes the chronicle's style considerably. It's usually only done for very important, tragic characters.

**A Note for the Storyteller:**  
Final Death should be a meaningful event. Don't throw it around lightly. Let every such loss leave a mark on the history of the coterie and the domain.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
Torpor isn't just "knocked out for a few nights." It's a time of nightmares, visions, conversations with the inner Beast, and painful memories. Many ancient vampires spend decades in torpor, reflecting on the meaning of their unlife.

---

### Ghouls

Ghouls are mortals (more rarely animals) who regularly drink a vampire's blood. They occupy a special place between human and Kindred: no longer entirely human, but still far from being a vampire.

Ghouls are one of the most important tools of power in the world of the night. Many Princes, elders, and influential coteries keep entire networks of ghouls as servants, bodyguards, spies, and managers of their daytime affairs.

#### What a Ghoul Is

A ghoul is a person (or animal) who **regularly** consumes vampire vitae. Even a small amount of blood grants them supernatural advantages, but in exchange creates a powerful dependency.

**Main Differences from an Ordinary Person:**

- Significantly slowed aging (practically stops aging, as long as they keep receiving blood).
- Enhanced physical traits.
- Rapid wound regeneration.
- Gains **1 level** in one of their master's (Domitor's) Disciplines.
- Strong psychological and physiological dependence on vitae.

A ghoul remains mortal: they can eat ordinary food, walk by day, have children, and die of old age or of wounds (though they heal faster).

#### Creating a Ghoul (the Ritual)

**How It Happens:**

1. The vampire must give their **vitae** to a mortal (usually 1–3 Blood points at a time).
2. The mortal drinks the vampire's blood.
3. The effect is almost immediate: a surge of strength, euphoria, and a powerful attraction to the vampire.

**How Much Blood Is Needed:**

- To **create** a ghoul, a single act of feeding is enough.
- To **sustain** a ghoul, they need to receive blood regularly (usually 1 Blood point about once every 3–4 weeks, depending on the ghoul's activity and age).

**Consent:**

- Consent is **not required**, mechanically speaking. Blood can be forced into someone's mouth, or Dominate can be used.
- However, **morally**, this is always a grave violation. Creating a ghoul against their will often results in a loss of Humanity (Stains).
- Most ghouls eventually become willing — owing to a powerful emotional and addictive dependence on vitae.

**Note:**  
Creating a ghoul is considered a **serious act** in Kindred society. The Camarilla requires the Prince's permission to create a ghoul within their domain. Many Anarchs are more relaxed about it, but still see ghouls as a potential danger.

---

#### Changes That Happen to a Person

When a mortal begins regularly drinking a vampire's vitae, they stop being an ordinary human. The process of becoming a ghoul affects both body and mind.

##### Physiological Changes

- **Slowed or halted aging** — as long as the ghoul keeps receiving vitae, the aging process almost completely stops. Many ghouls still look 25–40 years old even after 80–100 years of service.
- **Enhanced regeneration** — wounds heal 2–3 times faster than for an ordinary person. Serious injuries are still dangerous, but a ghoul is far hardier.
- **Increased physical traits** — Strength, Dexterity, and Stamina usually rise by 1–2 points (depending on the master's Blood Potency and length of service).
- **Improved immunity** — a ghoul resists disease, poisons, and toxins far better.
- **Dependence on vitae** — without regular doses of blood, a ghoul begins to age rapidly (sometimes within weeks), and suffers severe physical and psychological withdrawal.

##### Psychological and Emotional Changes

- **Strong emotional attachment** to their Domitor (the vampire master). Even without a full Blood Bond, a ghoul often feels deep loyalty, love, or obsession.
- **A shift in how they perceive the world** — over time, a ghoul comes to understand the nightside world better, growing more cynical, harder, and less squeamish.
- **Risk of mental decline** — after a long deprivation of vitae, or betrayal by their master, a ghoul may go insane, fall into depression, or become aggressive.
- **Loss of part of their humanity** — many ghouls gradually lose their moral compass, especially if their master is cruel or manipulative.

##### Benefits of Being a Ghoul

- **Access to a Discipline** — a ghoul gains **1 level** in one of their Domitor's Disciplines (chosen at creation, or upon the first dose of blood).
- **Significant longevity** — with regular feeding, they can live for centuries.
- **Increased physical power** — they become noticeably stronger, faster, and hardier than an ordinary person.
- **The ability to act by day** — the chief advantage over full-blooded Kindred.
- **Heightened senses** — many ghouls report that the world seems brighter, food tastes better, and emotions run stronger (at least at first).

**Important Limitation:**  
A ghoul **remains mortal**. They can die from severe wounds, disease, or old age (if deprived of vitae). They're also vulnerable to effects that don't work on vampires (certain poisons or diseases, for example).

---

#### Dependence on Vitae

The heaviest and most dangerous part of being a ghoul is **dependence on vampire blood**. It's far stronger than ordinary drug addiction, and has a mystical nature.

##### How the Dependence Works

- A ghoul **cannot** function normally without regular doses of vitae.
- Dependence begins almost immediately after the first dose, and intensifies with each new one.
- Unlike the **Blood Bond**, a ghoul's dependence is first and foremost a **physiological and psychological** withdrawal, not a mystical submission of will (though one often reinforces the other).

**The Difference from the Blood Bond:**
- The Blood Bond is **mystical slavery** (an emotional and volitional attachment).
- A ghoul's dependence is **chemical and physiological** withdrawal. A ghoul may hate their Domitor, and still crawl back to them for blood.

##### What Happens When Deprived of Blood

| Time Without Vitae     | Consequences |
|---------------------|-----------|
| 1–2 weeks          | Severe weakness, depression, irritability, insomnia |
| 3–4 weeks          | Rapid aging (can age 10–30 years in a month), severe pain, aggression |
| 1–2 months          | Extreme exhaustion, loss of most supernatural benefits, possible coma |
| More than 2–3 months   | Death (the body breaks down) |

When abruptly deprived of blood, a ghoul experiences a **withdrawal** comparable to severe heroin withdrawal, but with additional mystical effects (nightmares, hallucinations, a feeling that "something is dying inside").

##### How Dependence Develops Over Time

- **The first few months** — a strong craving, but still resistible.
- **After 1–2 years** — dependence becomes severe. A ghoul will go to great lengths for the next dose.
- **After 5–10 years** — most ghouls can no longer imagine life without vitae. Many become fully devoted to their Domitor.
- **Long-term service (20+ years)** — a ghoul often loses much of their former personality. Many become true fanatics, or broken instruments.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
Dependence is an excellent source of tragedy. A ghoul may hate their master with all their heart, yet be unable to betray them, because without blood they will simply die. This creates very powerful emotional conflicts.

**For the Storyteller:**  
Don't be afraid to use withdrawal as a story tool. Cutting off a ghoul's blood is one of the simplest and most effective ways to put pressure on their vampire master.

---

#### A Ghoul's Place in Kindred Society

Ghouls occupy a strange, in-between place in the world of the night. They're no longer quite human, but still very far from true Kindred. To most vampires, a ghoul is a **useful tool**, an "upgraded servant," a "pet," or a "loyal dog."

##### General Attitude

- Ghouls are considered **second-class** beings.
- They're often called "halfbloods," "blood slaves," "fed pets," or simply "ghouls."
- Even a loyal, useful ghoul is rarely seen as an equal. At best, they're treated as valuable property.
- Killing another vampire's ghoul is considered a serious insult, but not as grave a crime as killing a Kindred.

##### Attitudes of Different Sects and Clans

- **The Camarilla**  
  Ghouls are officially permitted, but strictly regulated. Creating a new ghoul usually requires the Prince's or Primogen's permission. Ghouls are considered the **property** of their Domitor. Killing another's ghoul can lead to a fine, compensation, or a blood feud.

- **The Anarchs**  
  A freer, more varied attitude. Some Anarchs see ghouls as "comrades in the struggle," or even as near-equals. Others use them as disposable resources. It all depends on the specific coterie and Baron.

- **The Sabbat**  
  Ghouls are treated as cannon fodder. They're created often, and used mercilessly. Long-lived ghouls are rare in the Sabbat — they're either quickly Embraced, or drained dry.

- **The Ministry (Setites)**  
  Masters at using ghouls for seduction, espionage, and control. Often create ghouls out of former lovers or cult devotees. Their attitude is pragmatic and predatory.

- **The Tremere**  
  Use ghouls mostly as lab assistants, guards, and ritual aides. Their attitude is cold and utilitarian.

- **The Nosferatu**  
  Often have the most devoted ghouls. Many "rats" trust their ghouls more than they trust other Kindred.

- **Thin-bloods and Caitiff**  
  For them, having even a single ghoul is already a mark of status and success. Many thin-bloods dream of a ghoul as a first step toward a "normal" unlife.

##### Legal Status

- **The Camarilla**: Ghouls are the Domitor's legal property. Killing or kidnapping one can be treated as a violation of the Traditions (especially Domain and Accounting).
- **The Anarchs**: There's almost no unified law. Everything is decided by the strength of the coterie and the Baron.
- **The Sabbat**: Ghouls hold an extremely low status. Their lives are valued very cheaply.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
A ghoul is an excellent source of personal drama. They might be the most devoted being in the world... or nurse a terrible, years-long grudge over a lifetime of humiliation and dependence. Many of the best stories in V5 are built around the complicated relationship between a vampire and their ghoul.

---

#### Transferring a Ghoul to Another Kindred

Yes, a ghoul **can be transferred** to another vampire, but the process is considered difficult, dangerous, and often painful — both for the ghoul, and for both Domitors involved.

##### Can It Be Done?

**Technically — yes.**  
**Practically — difficult and risky.**

In the Camarilla, such a transfer usually requires the Prince's permission, or at least formal notice. Among the Anarchs, it's all settled by force, agreement, or threats. The Sabbat takes a simpler view — ghouls there are often considered disposable.

##### How It Works Mechanically

There are two main methods:

1. **Gradual transfer** (the recommended, relatively safe method)  
   - The new Domitor begins regularly giving the ghoul their own blood.  
   - The old Domitor gradually reduces feedings down to zero.  
   - Within a few weeks (sometimes months), the old master's blood is fully replaced.  
   - The ghoul passes under the new Domitor's control.

2. **Abrupt transfer** (a fast, but dangerous method)  
   - The new Domitor gives the ghoul a large dose of their blood in a short span of time.  
   - The old Domitor stops feeding entirely.  
   - The process causes severe withdrawal.

##### Consequences of the Transfer

**For the Ghoul:**
- Severe physical and psychological withdrawal (which can last for weeks).
- Possible temporary loss of some benefits and Discipline access.
- Strong emotional trauma — many ghouls see a change of master as a betrayal, or as being sold off.
- Risk of death or insanity if the transfer is too abrupt.

**For the Former Master:**
- Loss of a valuable servant, bodyguard, or source of Discipline access.
- Possible loss of Humanity (especially if the ghoul had served them for many years).
- Risk of future revenge from the ghoul.

**For the New Master:**
- Gains an already-trained, experienced ghoul.
- Risk that the ghoul retains loyalty to, or harbors a deep grudge against, the previous master.
- The need to "retrain" the ghoul to serve them.

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
Transferring a ghoul is almost always a powerful dramatic scene. The ghoul may beg not to be given away, fly into a rage, sink into apathy, or attempt suicide. It's an excellent moment to show just how deeply a ghoul is attached to their Domitor.

**A Note for the Storyteller:**  
Transferring a ghoul is a powerful tool for creating conflict between characters. It's especially painful when a ghoul has been loyal to one vampire for years, only to be "sold" or traded away like an object.

---

**Limits and Practical Advice**

#### How Many Ghouls Can One Kindred Keep?

In theory — as many as they like.  
In practice — it depends heavily on resources and the style of the game:

- **An ordinary neonate** — 0–2 ghouls.
- **An ancilla / average vampire** — 2–5 ghouls.
- **An elder / Prince** — may keep 10–30+ ghouls (often an entire network of servants).

The more ghouls you have, the harder it is to feed, control, and hide them from prying eyes.

#### Does It Cost Experience?

- **Creating a ghoul** — **no** experience is required. Giving blood is enough.
- **Developing a ghoul** — yes, ghouls can advance with experience, like ordinary characters (though more slowly).  
  The Storyteller usually allows experience to be spent raising Attributes, Skills, and even additional levels of the master's Discipline.

#### Can a Ghoul Be Leveled Up?

Yes. A ghoul is a full (if simplified) character.  
They can:
- Raise their Attributes and Skills.
- Gain new Specialties.
- Improve their existing access to their master's Discipline (up to a certain limit).
- Over time, become a very dangerous and capable helper.

#### Risks and Typical Problems

- **Withdrawal** — if a ghoul goes too long without blood, they decline rapidly and may die.
- **Betrayal** — a wronged or abandoned ghoul is one of a vampire's most dangerous enemies.
- **The Masquerade** — ghouls know too much. Their death or disappearance often draws unwanted attention.
- **Jealousy and conflict** — ghouls can grow jealous of their master's other ghouls or Kindred.
- **Political consequences** — in the Camarilla, a large number of ghouls can be read as preparation for war, or a disruption of the balance of power.

**Tip for the Player:**  
Take your ghouls seriously. They can become the most loyal beings in your unlife... or the most terrifying avengers, if you betray them.

**Tip for the Storyteller:**  
Ghouls are an excellent source of drama. One loyal ghoul can become a tragic figure, and one wronged ghoul can become the catalyst for an entire story arc.

## 07. Disciplines
### General Rules for Using Disciplines

Disciplines are the supernatural abilities granted to a vampire by their blood. They are one of the Kindred's chief advantages over mortals.

#### Basic Activation Mechanics

- Most Disciplines activate **instantly**, and don't require a separate action in combat (unless stated otherwise).
- Activation usually costs **1 Blood point** (a Rouse Check).
- In a single turn, you can activate **only one power per Discipline**.
- Some powers require a **Rouse Check** (a die roll to see whether the power activates successfully).

#### The Rouse Check

When a power requires a Rouse Check:
- Roll **1d10**.
- Success is a 6 or higher.
- On a failure, the power either doesn't trigger, or triggers with complications (the Storyteller decides).

#### Amalgams

An **Amalgam** is a combined power that requires **two different Disciplines** at a specific level.

- Example: "Irresistible Voice" requires **Presence 4** + **Dominate 1**.
- Amalgams are considered very powerful, and very rare, powers.

#### Bonuses from Blood Potency

The higher a character's **Blood Potency**, the stronger their Disciplines perform:
- A bonus is added to the activation or resistance pool.
- The duration and strength of certain effects increase.
- Higher maximum trait values, and a larger amount of blood that can be spent per turn.

#### Limits and the Threat to the Masquerade

- Many Disciplines (especially Protean, Potence, and Celerity) carry a **high risk to the Masquerade**.
- The Storyteller may impose a penalty or complications if you use supernatural powers in front of mortals.
- Some powers (such as Presence or Dominate) look more "natural," and carry less risk.

#### Learning New Disciplines

- An **in-clan Discipline** (native to your clan) is learned more easily and cheaply (new rating × 5 experience).
- An **out-of-clan Discipline** is considerably more expensive (new rating × 7 experience).
- **Caitiff** learn every Discipline at a cost of × 6.
- To unlock a new Discipline, you must **at least once** taste the blood of a vampire who possesses it.

**Important Rule:**  
You cannot learn a Discipline above your current **Blood Potency** level.

### List of Disciplines (Alphabetical Order)

#### Animalism

**Brief Description**  
Animalism is power over the animal side of nature. The Discipline allows a vampire to understand, control, and use animals, and to sense the "Beast" within other creatures.

**Resonance**  
Choleric (anger, aggression) and Phlegmatic (calm, instinct) significantly enhance its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
Low / Moderate (depends on how openly you use the powers in front of mortals).

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Animalism powers require **1 Blood point** (automatically raising your Hunger by 1). Some powers also require a roll.

**Level 1**
- **Bond Famulus** — You form a mystical bond with one animal. Requires no roll and no blood. The animal becomes devoted, and understands simple commands.
- **Sense the Beast** — A passive ability. You automatically sense the Beast's presence in other creatures. Requires no activation.

**Level 2**
- **The Beckoning** — You let out an inaudible call. Requires **1 Blood point**. Animals of a chosen species in the area come to you. No roll required.
- **Sermo Bestiae** — Direct communication with animals. Requires **1 Blood point**. Roll **Charisma + Animalism** (difficulty 3–5 depending on the request).

**Level 3**
- **Subsume the Spirit** — You enter an animal's mind and fully control it. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll of **Resolve + Animalism** (difficulty depends on the animal).
- **Quell the Beast** — You suppress the Beast in another vampire or human. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll of **Resolve + Animalism** against the target's **Composure + Resolve**.

**Level 4**
- **Spirit of the Wild Pack** — You can simultaneously control a large pack of animals. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll.

**Level 5**
- **Ascendant the Beast** — You fully merge with your Beast, gaining enormous power but losing control of your mind. Requires **2 Blood points**, and usually leads to Frenzy.

**Amalgams**
- **Feral Whispers** (Animalism 3 + Auspex 2) — Allows you to sense an animal's emotions and intentions as clearly as you would a human's.

#### Presence

**Brief Description**  
Presence is power over the emotions and attention of those around you. Unlike Dominate, which overwhelms the will, Presence makes others **feel** what the vampire wants them to feel: admiration, fear, love, or awe. It's one of the most social, and most "human," of the Disciplines.

**Resonance**  
Sanguine (passion, excitement, enthusiasm) significantly enhances its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
Low / Moderate (most effects look natural, but some powers can be noticeable).

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Presence powers require **1 Blood point** (automatically raising your Hunger by 1). Some powers also require a roll.

**Level 1**
- **Awe** — You radiate powerful charisma. Everyone nearby is inclined to agree with you, and feels drawn to you. Requires no roll. Passive for the whole scene.
- **Daunt** — You appear dangerous and imposing. +1–2 dice to Intimidation. Requires no roll.

**Level 2**
- **Lingering Kiss** — A victim you've fed from becomes fixated on that sensation. Requires **1 Blood point**. The effect lasts until their next feeding.

**Level 3**
- **Entrancement** — You inspire blind devotion in a single target. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll of **Charisma + Presence** against the target's **Composure + Wits**.
- **Dread Gaze** — You reveal the Beast's true nature. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll of **Resolve + Presence**. Mortals flee in panic, or freeze.

**Level 4**
- **Summon** — You can mentally call a person or vampire who has previously been under your Presence. Requires **1 Blood point**. The target feels an overwhelming urge to come to you.
- **Irresistible Voice** — An **Amalgam** (Presence 4 + Dominate 1). You can use Dominate powers through voice alone, without eye contact.

**Level 5**
- **Star Magnet** — Your Presence powers now work through broadcasts, recordings, and live streams. Requires **1 Blood point**.
- **Majesty** — You become the embodiment of a god or a nightmare. Requires **2 Blood points**. Those around you can barely act against your will.

**Amalgams**
- **Irresistible Voice** (Presence 4 + Dominate 1)
- **Spark of Rage** (Presence 3 + Animalism 3) — instantly provokes a crowd or a single vampire into a frenzy.

#### Dominate

**Brief Description**  
Dominate is absolute control over a victim's mind and will. Unlike Presence, which manipulates emotion, Dominate overwhelms another's will, turning the victim into an obedient puppet. It's one of the most "tyrannical," and dangerous, of the Disciplines.

**Resonance**  
Phlegmatic (calm, cold calculation, submission) significantly enhances its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
Moderate / High (many commands look strange or suspicious to onlookers).

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Dominate powers require **eye contact** and a clear verbal command. Almost all powers require **1 Blood point**.

**Level 1**
- **Compel** — A one-word order, lasting one turn ("Stop!", "Be silent!", "Run!"). Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll of **Charisma + Dominate** against the target's **Composure + Wits**.
- **Cloud Memory** — Erases the last few minutes from a victim's memory. Requires **1 Blood point**.

**Level 2**
- **Mesmerize** — A complex instruction with no conditions ("Go home and burn all your records"). Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll.
- **Dementation** — An **Amalgam** (Dominate 2 + Obfuscate 2). In the course of a conversation, you can unravel a target's psyche, inducing stress or madness.

**Level 3**
- **The Forgetful Mind** — You plant a trigger in the subconscious ("When you see the Prince, tell him this…"). Requires **1 Blood point** + time to explain it.
- **Deep memory editing** — You fully replace or erase memories. Requires time and a roll.

**Level 4**
- **Rationalize** — The victim genuinely believes they carried out the order of their own free will, and finds justifications for it.

**Level 5**
- **Terminal Decree** — Lifts the ban on suicidal and self-harming commands.
- **Mass Manipulation** — You can use Dominate powers on an entire group of people at once.

**Amalgams**
- **Irresistible Voice** (Dominate 1 + Presence 4) — Using Dominate through voice alone, without eye contact.
- **Dementation** (Dominate 2 + Obfuscate 2)

#### Protean

**Brief Description**  
Protean is total power over one's own body and its shape. The Discipline allows flesh to be adapted to the needs of the hunt, survival, or war. It's one of the wildest, most frightening Disciplines, especially popular among the Gangrel.

**Resonance**  
Choleric (anger, aggression, instinct) and Melancholic (survival instinct, secrecy) significantly enhance its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
**High** — most powers are obviously inhuman, and easily expose a vampire's nature.

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Protean powers require **1 Blood point**. Some transformations remain active until the end of the scene, or until you return to your normal form.

**Level 1**
- **Eyes of the Beast** — Your eyes glow red in darkness. You see perfectly in total darkness. Requires no roll.
- **Weight of a Feather** — You become almost weightless. You take no damage from falls, and don't trigger pressure sensors.

**Level 2**
- **Feral Weapons** — You grow claws, or your fangs lengthen. +2 damage, and it deals **Aggravated damage** to mortals. Requires **1 Blood point**.

**Level 3**
- **Earth Meld** — You literally "dive" into the soil, becoming immune to the sun and to most attacks for the day. Only works on natural earth. Requires **1 Blood point**.
- **Shapechange** — You can transform into a wolf, or another large predatory beast roughly your size.

**Level 4**
- **Metamorphosis** — You can take the forms of smaller animals (a bat, a rat, a snake, an insect).

**Level 5**
- **Mist Form** — You transform into a cloud of mist. You become immune to physical attacks, and can seep through cracks. Vulnerable only to fire and the sun. Requires **2 Blood points**.
- **Unfettered Heart** — Your heart shifts position within your body. A stake to the heart is nearly impossible (+3 difficulty). If a stake is driven in anyway, you can try to force it back out.

**Amalgams**
- **Blood Blade** (Protean 2 + Blood Sorcery 3) — You create a weapon out of your own blood.
- **The Beast Within** (Protean 4 + Animalism 3) — An enhanced version of merging with an animal.

#### Auspex

**Brief Description**  
Auspex is the sharpening of supernatural senses and perception. The Discipline allows a vampire to see what's hidden, read auras, sense emotions, and even catch fragments of the future. It's one of the most "intellectual," and most useful, Disciplines for reconnaissance and intrigue.

**Resonance**  
Melancholic (sadness, focus, clarity) significantly enhances its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
Low / Moderate (most powers look like heightened observation, but some effects can be noticeable).

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Auspex powers are **passive**, or activate without blood. Some require **1 Blood point** + a roll.

**Level 1**
- **Heightened Senses** — You can sharply enhance one of your senses (sight, hearing, smell, and so on). Passively grants +1–2 dice to perception.
- **Premonition** — You occasionally receive brief visions, or intuitive flashes, of danger or important events.

**Level 2**
- **Scry the Soul** — You see a creature's aura, and can determine their emotional state, whether they're lying, or their supernatural nature. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll of **Intelligence + Auspex** against the target's **Composure + Resolve**.

**Level 3**
- **Share the Senses** — You can see and hear through the eyes and ears of another creature (human or vampire) you've previously touched.
- **Spirit's Touch** — You can "read" an object's history by touching it.

**Level 4**
- **Telepathy** — You can read a target's surface thoughts, or project your own thoughts to them. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll.
- **Clairvoyance** — You can mentally "look in" on a remote location you've seen before.

**Level 5**
- **Psychic Projection** — You can fully separate your mind from your body and travel in astral form.
- **Fate's Insight** — You receive powerful visions of the future, or of hidden connections.

**Amalgams**
- **Feral Whispers** (Auspex 2 + Animalism 3) — Allows you to read an animal's emotions and intentions.
- **Spirit's Perception** (Auspex 4 + Obfuscate 3) — You can see hidden or invisible creatures.

**Brief Description**  
Fortitude is supernatural endurance and resilience of body and mind. The Discipline allows a vampire to withstand monstrous damage, ignore pain, and resist mental attacks. It's one of the most "tank-like," defensive Disciplines.
Fortitude is supernatural endurance and resilience of body and mind. The Discipline allows a vampire to withstand monstrous damage, ignore pain, and resist mental attacks. It's one of the most "tank-like," defensive Disciplines.

**Resonance**  
Melancholic (endurance, fortitude, melancholy) and Phlegmatic (cool-headedness, calm) significantly enhance its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
Low (most effects look like incredible toughness or willpower).

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Fortitude powers are **passive** or activate automatically. Some require **1 Blood point**.

**Level 1**

#### Celerity

**Brief Description**  
Celerity is supernatural speed, grace, and reflexes. The Discipline allows a vampire to move faster than the human eye can follow, land a flurry of strikes in a fraction of a second, and perform impossible acrobatic feats.

**Resonance**  
Choleric (anger, excitement, reaction speed) significantly enhances its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
**Moderate / High** — a vampire's movements become obviously inhuman, especially at higher levels.

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Celerity powers require **1 Blood point**. Many effects are passive, or activate instantly.

**Level 1**
- **Rapid Reflexes** — In a firefight, you don't suffer a penalty for lacking cover. You can take one minor action per turn with no penalty.
- **Cat's Grace** — You automatically keep your balance on any surface (ledges, wires, slick floors). Balance rolls are almost never required.

**Level 2**
- **Fleetness** — Requires **1 Blood point**. Adds your Celerity rating to Dexterity rolls (outside combat) and to your defense in combat.

**Level 3**
- **Blink** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You can instantly move up to 50 meters and take an action in the same turn. To onlookers, it looks like teleportation.
- **Traversal** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You can run up vertical walls, or sprint across the surface of water.

**Level 4**
- **Unerring Aim** — An **Amalgam** (Celerity 4 + Auspex 2). The difficulty of ranged attacks drops to 1. The target can barely dodge.
- **Draught of Elegance** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You can pass a portion of your Celerity to another creature that drinks your blood.

**Level 5**
- **Lightning Strike** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You land a close-combat strike at difficulty 1. The opponent can barely defend themselves.
- **Split Second** — Requires **2 Blood points**. You can "undo" or prevent an event that just happened (within the last few seconds).

**Amalgams**
- **Unerring Aim** (Celerity 4 + Auspex 2)
- **Dance of Death** (Celerity 5 + Potence 3) — Allows multiple attacks in a single turn with minimal penalties.

#### Obfuscate

**Brief Description**  
Obfuscate is the art of hiding, vanishing, and deceiving perception. Unlike Protean, which changes the body, Obfuscate acts on the minds of those around you, making them "not notice" you. It's one of the most useful Disciplines for espionage, escape, and covert action.

**Resonance**  
Melancholic (secrecy, detachment, inconspicuousness) significantly enhances its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
Low / Moderate (the powers work on perception, but the effect can collapse if you act aggressively).

**General Mechanics:**  
Most Obfuscate powers require **1 Blood point**. Some powers are passive, or activate automatically.

**Level 1**
- **Silence of Death** — You move in absolute silence. Suppresses the sound of footsteps and rustling clothes. Doesn't affect microphones.
- **Cloak of Shadows** — As long as you stand still and have at least minimal cover, you are extremely hard to notice.

**Level 2**
- **Unseen Passage** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You can move while remaining invisible. Cannot be activated if someone is already looking at you. Aggressive actions or loud noise break the effect.

**Level 3**
- **Mask of a Thousand Faces** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You don't vanish, but instead take on the appearance of an unremarkable person. Allows you to interact with people without drawing attention.
- **Ghost in the Machine** — Your Obfuscate powers work against cameras and surveillance systems in real time. On recordings, you appear blurred.

**Level 4**
- **Vanish** — Requires **1 Blood point**. You can activate invisibility right in front of observers. Mortals often forget they saw anyone at all.
- **Conceal** — An **Amalgam** (Obfuscate 4 + Auspex 3). You can hide an inanimate object, or a small room, from the perception of those around you.

**Level 5**
- **Soul Mask** — You can fully hide your aura and emotional state from Auspex.
- **Cloak the Gathering** — You can hide a small group of people at the same time.

**Amalgams**
- **Conceal** (Obfuscate 4 + Auspex 3)
- **Shadow Whisper** (Obfuscate 3 + Dominate 2) — Allows you to whisper commands from a distance while remaining unseen.

#### Blood Sorcery

**Brief Description**  
Blood Sorcery is an ancient blood magic that turns vitae into a weapon, a protective ward, a curse, or an elaborate ritual. Once the monopoly of the Tremere clan, this Discipline is now held by other Kindred as well. It's one of the most flexible, and strategically powerful, Disciplines in the game.

**Resonance**  
Phlegmatic (calm, focus) and Melancholic (sadness, longing, depth) significantly enhance its effects.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
**High** — most powers, and especially rituals, look obviously supernatural.

**General Mechanics**

Blood Sorcery is split into two types:

- **Powers** — fast, combat or support effects. Usually require **1 Blood point** and a roll.
- **Rituals** — lengthy ceremonies that require time (from 5 minutes to several hours), special components, blood, and a roll. Many rituals have a long-lasting effect.

**Learning:**  
To learn a new power or ritual, you spend experience (just like an ordinary Discipline). The Tremere learn them more easily.

---

**Blood Sorcery Powers**

**Level 1**
- **Corrosive Vitae** — You make your blood corrosive. You can use it as a weapon in close combat.
- **Extinguish Vitae** — You temporarily weaken or block another vampire's blood.

**Level 2**
- **Scourging** — You deal damage to an opponent at range, using their own blood, from within their body.
- **Ritual: Sunlight Protection** — One of the most popular rituals. Allows you to endure sunlight with less damage.

**Level 3**
- **Boil Blood** — You make the victim's blood literally boil, dealing Aggravated damage.
- **Ritual: Call of Blood** — You can draw blood from wounds or from containers.

**Level 4**
- **Blood Horror** — You turn the victim's own blood into a weapon against them, causing intense pain and fear.
- **Ritual: Ghoul Creation** — An enhanced version of the ghoul-creation ritual.

**Level 5**
- **Blood Stone** — You turn your blood into a powerful magical artifact with various effects.
- **Ritual: Total Submission** — One of the most dangerous rituals, capable of severely weakening a victim's will.

---

**Blood Sorcery Rituals**

Rituals are lengthy ceremonies. They require:
- Time (from 10 minutes to several hours)
- Components (blood, herbs, symbols, the victim's personal effects, and so on)
- A roll (usually Intelligence + Blood Sorcery)

**Popular Rituals:**

- **Sunlight Protection** (level 2) — Significantly reduces sunlight damage for several hours.
- **Ghoul Creation** (level 3–4) — Reliably turns a mortal into a ghoul.
- **Blood Contract** (level 3) — Creates a magically binding agreement between two parties.
- **Haven Protection** (level 2–4) — Sets up magical wards around your haven.
- **Summon Ghost** (level 4) — Summons and temporarily binds the spirit of someone deceased.

**Special Notes:**
- Rituals can be performed in advance, and the effect stored (such as protecting your haven).
- Many rituals require a **personal item** belonging to the target, or their blood, for maximum effectiveness.
- The Tremere are traditionally the finest practitioners of this Discipline.

**Amalgams**
- **Blood Blade** (Blood Sorcery 3 + Potence 2)
- **Blood Shield** (Blood Sorcery 4 + Fortitude 3)

Oblivion is a necromantic Discipline tied to shadow, the void, and the world of the dead. It allows a vampire to manipulate shadows, summon and bind ghosts, drain life force, and open rifts into Oblivion itself. Especially popular among the Hecata, the Lasombra, and necromancers.

**Threat to the Masquerade**  
**High** — almost all powers look obviously supernatural, and frightening.

**General Mechanics:**  
Oblivion is split into **powers** (fast effects) and **rituals** (lengthy ceremonies). Most powers require **1 Blood point**. Some rituals require components, time, and additional rolls.

**Level 1**
- **Shadow Sight** — You can see in total darkness, and notice ghosts. A passive ability.
- **Touch of Oblivion** — By touching a target, you drain part of their life force. Requires **1 Blood point**.

**Level 2**
- **Summon the Shadow** — You can manipulate shadows, making them solid, stretching them, or creating simple illusions. Requires **1 Blood point**.
- **Whispers from Beyond** — You can briefly communicate with the recently deceased. Requires **1 Blood point**.

**Level 3**
- **Drain Life** — You drain life force from a target at a distance, dealing Aggravated damage. Requires **1 Blood point** + a roll.

**Level 4**
- **Summon Wraith** — You can summon and temporarily bind the ghost of someone deceased. Requires **1 Blood point** + ritual components.

**Level 5**

Oblivion has fewer rituals than Blood Sorcery, but they're very powerful and thematic:

- **Seal of the Soul** (level 4) — Seals a soul or ghost inside an object.
- **Oblivion Rift** (level 5) — Opens a small portal into the world of the dead (very dangerous).

### Thin-Blood Alchemy

**What Alchemy Is**

Alchemy is a unique ability belonging only to thin-bloods (14th–16th generation). Instead of using full Disciplines, they've learned to **mimic** the effects of other powers through potions and concoctions made from blood, Resonance, and chemical substances.

This isn't magic in the classic sense, but rather **blood crafting**.

**How Alchemy Works**

1. You gather **ingredients**:
   - Blood with the needed **Resonance** (required)
   - Ordinary chemicals, herbs, metals, or personal items
2. You perform a **crafting ritual** (requires time, a lab, or an improvised setup).
3. You produce **one dose of the formula** (a Distillation).

**General Rules:**
- Every formula has a **level** (1–5).
- Crafting requires a roll of **Intelligence + Thin-Blood Alchemy**.
- A formula works **once** (one dose).
- A thin-blood can hold only a limited number of active formulas at once (usually 3–5).

---

#### List of Alchemy Formulas

**Level 1**

- **Quicksilver**  
  **Ingredients:** Blood with Sanguine or Choleric Resonance + mercury or stimulants.  
  **Prep time:** 15–30 minutes.

## 08. Game Rules

### The Basics of the Storyteller System

**Vampire: The Masquerade 5th Edition** uses the **Storyteller System**, based on ten-sided dice (d10). It's simple to learn, but capable of creating tense and dramatic moments.

#### How a Roll Works

1. Assemble a **dice pool** = Attribute + Skill (+ modifiers).
2. Roll all the dice at once.
3. **A success** is any die showing **6 or higher**.
4. **Difficulty** — the number of successes needed (usually 2 to 5).

#### Important Concepts

- **A win** — the number of successes ≥ the Difficulty.
- **Margin** — the difference between successes and the difficulty (used to calculate damage, the quality of the outcome, and so on).
- **A Bestial Success / Critical Win** — two 10s (10+10) = **+4 successes**.
- **A Triumph** — a win that includes at least one Critical pair (an especially vivid, striking success).
- **A Total Failure** — **zero successes** on the entire roll. The Storyteller may introduce serious complications.

#### The Red Hunger Dice (V5's Signature Feature)

When your **Hunger is 1 or higher**, some of your ordinary dice are replaced with **red Hunger dice**.

| Result on a Red Die | Effect |
|-------------------------|--------|
| **1**                   | **A Bestial Failure** — the Beast gets a chance to intervene |
| **6–9**                 | An ordinary success |
| **10**                  | **A Bestial Success** — a normal success, plus often an especially vivid or savage effect |

**Key Rules:**
- A 1 on a red die **does not cancel out** ordinary successes (unlike in V20).
- A Bestial Failure **does not automatically** fail the action, but it does give the Storyteller license to introduce a complication caused by the Beast.
- A Bestial Success usually makes a win more dramatic, or more dangerous.

#### When to Roll Dice, and When Not To

**V5's Golden Rule:**

> "If a roll carries no risk, or isn't interesting, don't roll it."

Only roll when:
- There is **real risk** of failure.
- The outcome is **not obvious**.
- Failure or success **matters** to the story.

Don't roll if the action is trivial for the character, or if a failure would ruin the fun of the game.

#### Willpower and Blood

- **Spending Willpower** — allows you to **reroll up to 3 ordinary dice** (not the red ones). Costs 1 Willpower point (mild stress).
- **Blood** — **does not** grant +1 success directly. It is spent mainly to activate Disciplines and for Rouse Checks.

#### A Quick Player Cheat Sheet

| Situation                              | Result |
|---------------------------------------|---------|
| No successes at all                    | Total Failure |
| You have the required number of successes        | Success |
| 1 on a red die                   | A Bestial Failure (the Beast may intervene) |
| 10 on a red die                  | A Bestial Success (often especially striking/savage) |
| A Bestial Failure + ordinary successes      | The action is resolved by the ordinary successes + a complication from the Beast |

---

### Contests

A Contest is used when **two characters** (or a character and the world) directly oppose one another. One side's success automatically means the other's failure.

#### When a Contest Is Used

- One character is trying to do something, while another (or circumstance itself) **actively works against them**.
- Examples:
  - You try to sneak past a guard → they try to spot you.
  - You try to deceive the Prince → they try to catch the lie.
  - You try to break free of a grapple → your opponent tries to hold on.
  - You try to pick a lock → the alarm system or the homeowner tries to stop you.

#### How a Contest Works

Each participant assembles their own **dice pool** and rolls at the same time.

- **Victory** goes to whoever has **more successes**.
- On a tie, **the initiator wins** (whoever started the action first).

#### The Half Success Rule

To keep the game from bogging down, especially for NPC rolls, the Storyteller may use a simplified rule:

- Instead of rolling, the NPC's pool is divided **in half** (rounded down).
- The resulting number counts as their number of successes.

**Example:**  
A guard has a pool of 6 dice.  
The Storyteller divides 6 / 2 = **3 successes**.  
You need to roll **4 or more** successes to get past them.

This rule is especially useful in crowd scenes, and when many NPCs are involved.

**Tip for the Storyteller:**  
Use a full Contest for important confrontations (especially between players and key NPCs). Use the Half Success Rule for rank-and-file opponents and background actions, to keep the pace from dragging.

---

### Conflicts

A Conflict is a Contest in which the participants can deal each other **damage** (physical, social, or mental). It's the most tense, dramatic moment in the game.

#### Types of Conflict

| Conflict Type     | What's Defended          | What's Dealt          | Examples |
|-------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|-------|
| **Physical**    | Health       | Damage            | A fight, a shootout, a chase |
| **Social**    | Willpower        | Stress                 | An argument, intimidation, manipulation |
| **Mental**    | Willpower                    | Stress                 | Psychological pressure, torture, Dominate |

#### Turns and Initiative

- A Conflict is divided into **turns** (rounds).
- In each turn, every participant takes **one significant action**.
- **Initiative** is usually **narrative** (the Storyteller describes the order of actions according to the scene's logic). When needed, you can roll **Composure + Awareness**.

#### Attacks, Defense, and Maneuvers

- **An Attack** — an active action meant to deal damage/stress.
- **Defense** — passive resistance (uses your full pool).
- **A Maneuver** — any non-standard action (taking cover, repositioning, distracting an opponent, and so on).

**Important Rules:**
- When attacking multiple targets, the pool is split between them in any proportion.
- When defending against multiple opponents, the pool is reduced by **1d10** for each opponent beyond the first.

#### Multiple Actions

You can take **only one significant action** per turn.

- **Attacking multiple targets in one turn** is possible, but requires **splitting your dice pool**.  
  Example: an 8-die pool → 5 against the first enemy, 3 against the second. Each roll is resolved separately.
- **Minor actions** (moving a couple of steps, reloading, drawing a weapon, a short command) are possible, but each one imposes a **-1d10 penalty** on your main roll.

**The Rule of Three Turns**

To keep conflicts from dragging on, it's recommended to try to resolve any conflict within **three turns**.  
If no winner has emerged after three turns, the Storyteller can offer a **compromise**, or move to a **Blitz Conflict** (one decisive roll).

**Tip for the Storyteller:**  
Try to describe conflicts dynamically and cinematically. Don't drag a fight out past three or four turns — it's better to move on to narrative consequences.

**Tip for Players:**  
Use maneuvers, the environment, and your Desires — they're often more effective than direct attacks.

---

### Damage, Health, and Willpower

In V5, a character has two main tracks: **Health** and **Willpower**. They show how physically and morally capable a character is of continuing to act.

#### The Health and Willpower Tracks

- **Health** — a character's physical integrity.  
  Base value = Stamina + 3.
- **Willpower** — mental and emotional fortitude.  
  Base value = Composure + Resolve.

Both tracks have **7–10 boxes** (depending on traits). Each box can hold:
- **Superficial damage** [/] — surface wounds / stress.
- **Aggravated damage** [X] — serious wounds / a deep psychological blow.

#### Superficial and Aggravated Damage

| Damage Type       | Notation | How It Works for Vampires | Examples |
|-----------------|-------------|--------------------------|-------|
| **Superficial** | [/]         | Halved (rounded up) | Gunshot wounds, blows, minor stress |
| **Aggravated** | [X]         | Dealt in full      | Fire, sunlight, werewolf claws, betrayal |

#### How Damage Is Dealt

- **A Physical Conflict** → damage to the **Health** track.
- **A Social / Mental Conflict** → damage to the **Willpower** track (stress).

The **Margin** of a Contest = base damage.  
Modifiers are then added (weapons, environment, Disciplines).

#### Healing and Recovery

**Superficial damage:**
- Vampires heal **1 point per turn** through a **Rouse Check** (spending 1 Blood point).
- At the start of a session, everyone recovers Health points equal to their **Stamina**.

**Aggravated damage:**
- Vampires heal **1 point per day** (requires blood).
- Mortals heal much more slowly (weeks in the hospital).

**Willpower:**
- At the start of a session, points equal to **Resolve or Composure** are recovered.
- 1 point can be recovered if the character actively worked toward their **Ambition**.

#### Impairment and Critical State

- **Impairment** — when a track is completely full (with any kind of damage).  
  The character suffers **-2d10** on all rolls of the matching type.
- **Overflow** — every new point of damage turns one Superficial marker [/] into an Aggravated one [X].
- **Critical State** — a track is completely filled with Aggravated damage:
  - **Health** → a vampire falls into **Torpor**; a mortal falls into a coma, or dies.
  - **Willpower** → the character is completely broken (may lose consciousness, surrender, or fall into hysterics).

**Roleplaying Tip:**  
Don't be afraid to take damage — it's part of the drama. Superficial damage is relatively safe for vampires, but Aggravated damage is already a serious threat. Use descriptions of wounds and stress to make scenes more cinematic.

---

### Hunger and Rouse Checks

**Hunger** is V5's central mechanic, replacing the old Blood Pool system. It makes the game more dangerous and bestial: the higher your Hunger, the more the Beast affects you.

#### The Hunger Pool in Rolls

When your **Hunger is 1 or higher**, some of the ordinary dice in any roll are replaced with **red Hunger dice**.

- Hunger 0 → all dice are ordinary
- Hunger 1 → 1 red die
- Hunger 2 → 2 red dice
- ...
- Hunger 5 → 5 red dice (the maximum)

Red dice work like ordinary ones, but carry special effects (see the "Red Hunger Dice" section above).

#### The Rouse Check

Most Disciplines, and some actions, require a **Rouse Check**.

**How It's Done:**
- Roll **1 ordinary d10**.
- Success is **6 or higher**.
- **On a failure** — you spend **1 Blood point**, and Hunger rises by 1.
- **On a success** — no blood is spent.

The higher your current Hunger, the more often you'll fail Rouse Checks.

#### Frenzy

When the pressure grows too great, the Beast can take control of the character.

**Types of Frenzy:**

| Type of Frenzy     | When It Usually Happens                  | What Happens to the Character |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Rage Frenzy** | Taking Aggravated damage, severe hunger, provocation | A furious attack on everything nearby |
| **Fear Frenzy** | Severe stress, humiliation, loss of Willpower   | Flight, or complete apathy |
| **Hunger Frenzy** | Extreme hunger + a failed Rouse Check  | A mad search for blood |

**How Frenzy Begins:**
- Automatically, under certain triggers (especially at Hunger 4–5).
- Or after **failing a resistance roll** (usually **Composure + Resolve**).

**How to Come Out of Frenzy:**
- A successful Composure + Resolve roll (difficulty rises with Hunger).
- Satisfying the trigger (sating the Hunger with blood, removing the threat, and so on).
- Help from allies (social rolls).

**Tip for Players:**  
Frenzy isn't a punishment — it's part of the drama. A good player uses it to create vivid roleplaying moments.

**Tip for the Storyteller:**  
Don't overuse forced Frenzy, but make sure Hunger always feels dangerous. The red dice should scare your players.

### Extended Rules

#### Hunting and Feeding

Hunting is one of the most important things a Kindred does. In V5, it has become far more mechanized, and far more atmospheric.

**Predator Types in Action**

Every Kindred has their own **Predator Type**, which grants bonuses and drawbacks. While hunting, the player describes exactly how they hunt, and gains the corresponding bonuses.

**Finding Vessels and Difficulty**

| Hunting Conditions                     | Difficulty | Example |
|-----------------------------------|-----------|--------|
| A wealthy district, a busy night      | 2         | A club, a bar, a festival |
| An ordinary district                     | 3         | Streets, a park |
| A dangerous district / curfew | 4         | An industrial zone, a locked-down block |
| After the Second Inquisition, cameras everywhere   | 5+        | Almost any district |

**Hunting Mechanics:**
- Usually a single roll of **your Predator Type's bonus Skill + the relevant Skill** (Streetwise, Charisma, Stealth, and so on).
- On a success, you find a vessel and gain **1–3 Blood points** + a **Resonance**.
- On a failure, complications are possible (the police, a rival, bad Resonance, and so on).

**Resonance While Hunting**

The quality and type of blood heavily shape the game:
- **Sanguine** — enhances Presence and the social Disciplines.
- **Choleric** — enhances Animalism, Potence, Celerity.
- **Melancholic** — enhances Auspex, Oblivion, Fortitude.
- **Phlegmatic** — enhances Dominate, Blood Sorcery.

A strong Resonance (a Dyscrasia) grants an **additional bonus** to the matching Discipline.

#### Projects

Projects are a system for long-term actions (from several days to several months).

**How to Start a Project:**
1. Define the project's goal and scope.
2. The Storyteller sets a **difficulty level** (usually 3–6), and a **required number of successes** (10–30+).
3. For each "stretch of time" (a week, a month), you make a roll relevant to the project.
4. Accumulate successes until you reach the required total.

**Examples of Projects:**
- Build a network of informants
- Build/reinforce a haven
- Perform a complex ritual
- Develop a new alchemical formula

#### Other Extended Mechanics

- **Scene Scale** — the Storyteller can speed up or slow down time depending on how important a moment is.
- **Styles of Play**:
  - **Quick Conflict** — a single roll, instead of a full combat sequence.
  - **Focused Play** — emphasis on roleplay and intrigue.
- **The Influence of Environment and Equipment** — good gear or an advantageous position grants +1–3 dice. A poor environment imposes a penalty.
